# Synthetic Sales Conversation: Acme Manufacturing Discovery

Date: 2026-05-14
Account: Acme Manufacturing Group
Segment: Global manufacturing
Participants: Maya Chen, Director of Marketing Operations; Luis Romero, Revenue Enablement; Priya Shah, AI Program Lead; Alex Morgan, Seller

## Summary

Acme is piloting AI-assisted campaign briefs across four business units. The pilot is slowed by inconsistent product language, scattered proof points, and uncertainty about whether sales-call notes are approved for marketing use.

## Transcript Excerpt

Alex: What made context governance a priority now?

Maya: The first pilot looked impressive until we compared outputs across teams. One brief said we help "automate compliance approvals," another said "centralize all customer evidence," and a third pulled language from a call note that legal had never reviewed.

Priya: The model was not the issue. The issue was that every team gave it a different pile of context.

Luis: Sales has useful language, but a transcript should not become a marketing claim just because it ranked high in search. We want the field language for insight, not as approved proof.

Alex: How would you want that separated?

Maya: Approved guidance should come from a small set of records: positioning, proof points, disclaimers, and launch notes. Raw material should be searchable with citations, but visibly separate.

Priya: I also need to test the exact package the workflow will receive. A preview that summarizes things is not enough. I need the JSON or at least the actual records and passages.

Alex: What would make a pilot successful?

Maya: Three things. First, viewers can request context but cannot edit the source. Second, editors can keep folders and documents current without a ticket queue. Third, every Collection passage is cited so nobody mistakes it for approved truth.

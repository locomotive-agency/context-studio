# Context system messaging pillars

## Pillar 1: Governed context stays governed

Approved OKF Documents are curated records. They are retrieved deterministically by type, scope, status, criticality, and freshness. They are not blended into a semantic soup.

Use this pillar when the buyer is worried about legal language, brand standards, product claims, or executive-approved messaging.

## Pillar 2: Supporting sources stay searchable

Collections are for source material: transcripts, interviews, launch notes, competitive research, and campaign documents. They are searchable and cited, but they do not automatically become approved context.

Use this pillar when the buyer has hundreds of useful files but does not want to turn each file into an OKF record.

## Pillar 3: Teams can test the real tool response

The Tool Test Bench calls the same context package endpoint that AI workflows use. Users can inspect the request, response, approved context, cited passages, missing controlled context, and suggested supporting sources.

Use this pillar when the buyer asks how teams will know what an AI workflow actually received.

## Pillar 4: Permissions are easy to explain

Admins manage users and everything else. Editors manage Folders, Documents, and Collections. Viewers request context and semantic data. Every data path requires login.

Use this pillar when the buyer asks whether this will create another complex governance workflow.

## Avoid

- Do not claim the system replaces CMS, DAM, CRM, or data warehouse tools.
- Do not say raw sales transcripts are approved source of truth.
- Do not describe Collections as governed context records.
- Do not promise automated compliance approval.

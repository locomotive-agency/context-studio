# Competitive landscape: GTM Context Graph

## Direct competitors (head-to-head)

- [[oracle-data-cloud--unity-cdp|Oracle Fusion Unity Data Platform]] — strength: Unifies system-of-record data (orders/renewals/contracts/service/finance) with behavioral signals — "enterprise truth not marketing truth"; gap: Lists ZoomInfo as a B2B enrichment partner — Oracle is a data consumer at the activation layer.
- [[people-ai--backstory-revenue-answers|Backstory Revenue Answers Platform]] — strength: Nine-year proprietary activity dataset competitors can't replicate; gap: No third-party B2B contact data foundation — depends on customer's first-party activity.
- [[revenue-grid--activity-capture|Activity Capture (RG Capture)]] — strength: Indefinite retention positions directly against Einstein Activity Capture's 24-month cap; gap: Salesforce-native only — no HubSpot or Microsoft Dynamics support promoted.
- [[6sense--predictive-analytics|Predictive Analytics]] — strength: Funnel-stage prediction is more granular than ZoomInfo's intent-topic model; gap: Predictive model is funnel-stage focused — does not fuse first-party CRM + conversation intelligence + behavioral signals into a unified reasoning layer the way GTM Context Graph does.
- [[aviso-ai--revenue-forecasting|Revenue Forecasting]] — strength: Native consumption-based revenue forecasting alongside ACV; gap: No B2B data foundation underneath the forecast model.
- [[demandbase--account-intelligence|Demandbase Account Intelligence]] — strength: Strong account-level reasoning across intent + firmographics; gap: Less rich first-party signal fusion than GTM Context Graph (which fuses CRM + Chorus + behavioral signals).
- [[salesforce-einstein--einstein-predictive|Einstein (predictive AI for CRM)]] — strength: Decade-deep predictive AI maturity (launched 2016); gap: Predictive signals limited to first-party CRM data — no third-party intent / technographics.
- [[aviso-ai--platform|Aviso Platform (Revenue Operating System)]] — strength: Time-Series AI Engine (Replay) goes back 8 quarters tracking every field change; gap: Platform is internal infrastructure.
- [[aviso-ai--relationship-intelligence|Relationship Intelligence (Aviso Engage)]] — strength: Champion B/C multi-threading framework with automatic gap detection; gap: Relationship graph built from first-party signals — no third-party org-chart data layer.

## Adjacent competitors (overlap on some features only)

- [[acxiom--real-id|Real ID / Real Identity]] — strength: Six-decade consumer identity incumbent; gap: Consumer (B2C) identity — not B2B account/contact graph.
- [[alphasense--generative-search|Generative Search]] — strength: Premium financial document corpus (filings; gap: No verified B2B contact data underneath the reasoning layer.
- [[contify--m-and-ci-platform|Contify M&CI Platform]] — strength: 1 Mn+ vetted sources including 200k+ non-English (vs ZoomInfo's English-first signal layer); gap: No B2B contact / firmographic / technographic dataset attached to signals.
- [[alphasense--deep-research|Deep Research]] — strength: Autonomous research agent for analyst-grade briefings; gap: Briefings target investment/strategy decisions not GTM motion.
- [[crayon--aggregate|Crayon Aggregate]] — strength: Before/after change detection on competitor websites and pricing pages; gap: No B2B contact or firmographic data tied to signals.
- [[klaviyo--data-platform|Klaviyo Data Platform (KDP)]] — strength: Built-in CDP — no separate product or third-party stitch; gap: Profile graph is B2C consumer-level — no B2B account or contact identity.
- [[salesforce-sales-cloud--einstein|Einstein]] — strength: Native to Salesforce CRM — predictions tied to CRM data; gap: Bound by Salesforce data — lacks external intent + verified contact data + Chorus signals.
- [[alphasense--workflow-agents|Workflow Agents]] — strength: Push-style research delivery on schedule; gap: Agents push research deliverables not GTM actions.
- [[dreamdata--customer-journeys|Dreamdata Customer Journeys]] — strength: Out-of-the-box account-level journey timelines without manual modeling; gap: Visualization and analysis layer — not a reasoning/agent surface.
- [[informatica-data-quality--agentic-data-management|Informatica Agentic Data Management]] — strength: Trusted-data foundation for enterprise AI agents (agentic AI thesis); gap: Trust framework is metadata-driven across all enterprise data.
- [[dreamdata--data-platform|Dreamdata Data Platform]] — strength: Open warehouse access — free customer access to modeled data in BigQuery/Snowflake/Redshift/Azure/S3 for custom queries/BI/reverse ETL; gap: Does not maintain a contact/company database — BYO data from ZoomInfo/Cognism/Lusha/Apollo/etc..
- [[insightsquared--revops-dashboards|InsightSquared RevOps Dashboards]] — strength: 350+ out-of-the-box reports — largest pre-built library in revenue intelligence; gap: Reasoning is bounded to customer's first-party CRM + activity data.
- [[salesforce-einstein--einstein-relationship-insights|Einstein Relationship Insights]] — strength: Pulls public web + news + private Salesforce Files into one research surface; gap: No verified contacts / direct dials / mobile numbers.
- [[insightsquared--interactive-reporting|InsightSquared Interactive Reporting]] — strength: Confidence-to-Close + ICP ML models applied to pipeline review; gap: Pipeline reasoning bounded to first-party CRM + conversation data.

## Not actually competitors (reader confusion to address)

- Standalone intent platforms (Bombora, G2 Buyer Intent) — provide intent signals as inputs but are not reasoning layers.
- "Data layer" only competitors (Apollo B2B Data, Cognism, Clay Waterfall) — these compete with Pillar 1 (Data), not Pillar 2 (Context Graph). The frequent reader confusion is conflating data depth with reasoning depth.

## Spotlight selection guidance

For articles about predictive scoring or account intelligence: spotlight 6sense Predictive Analytics + Demandbase Account Intelligence. Lean into the unified reasoning layer: GTM Context Graph fuses B2B data + CRM + Chorus + behavioral signals — competitors typically reason on a narrower set (funnel stage only, or intent + firmographics only).

For articles about AI agents and what makes them trustworthy: lean into the Context Graph as the grounding layer that prevents hallucinated AI claims. Competitor agents (Apollo AI, Outreach AI Agents, Salesloft AI Agents) work without verified-data + cross-signal grounding — articles can frame this as the difference between "AI that knows your customers" and "AI that guesses."

For articles about CRM data quality vs. enrichment vs. intelligence: lean into the Context Graph as the layer above data quality, contrasted with the CRM-bound reasoning of Salesforce Einstein and HubSpot Breeze AI.

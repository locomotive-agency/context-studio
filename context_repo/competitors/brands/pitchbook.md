## Summary

PitchBook (a Morningstar company) is a private capital markets data, research, and technology platform sold primarily to PE/VC firms, investment banks, limited partners, credit investors, asset managers, and corporate development teams — a categorically different ICP from ZoomInfo's GTM audience. Direct overlap with ZoomInfo is narrow and limited to private-company financial data surfaced through PitchBook's CRM integration (Salesforce, Microsoft Dynamics, HubSpot) for business-development use cases; TrustRadius reviewers describe using both products concurrently, with ZoomInfo handling B2B contact accuracy and PitchBook handling funding / financial context. Most of PitchBook's surface area — fund returns benchmarking, LP commitments, deal sourcing for PE/VC/M&A, Lumonic portfolio monitoring — has no ZoomInfo equivalent, and most of ZoomInfo's surface area (verified contact data at scale, intent, GTM Workspace, GTM Studio, Chorus, MCP) has no PitchBook equivalent. PitchBook should be treated as a tangential, not head-to-head, competitor.

## Product Offerings

### PitchBook Platform (overview)

> **Wiki:** [[pitchbook--platform]]

- **Key features:**

  - Screening capabilities for investments, lending partners, and acquisitions with saved searches and alerts
  - Capital market profiles spanning companies, funds, investors, LPs, and people
  - Analyst-curated market analysis and verticals
  - Fund performance analysis (IRR, TVPI, DPI)
  - Capital market research covering 110,000+ public companies plus private analyst reports
- **Source URL:** https://pitchbook.com/products

### PitchBook Desktop

> **Wiki:** [[pitchbook--desktop]]

- **What it does:** The flagship workflow hub for the full investment lifecycle, with extensive datasets, advanced search, and analytical tools in one interface.

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:** Two narrow points of head-to-head competition. (1) Data foundation — companies / people: PitchBook's company and people data overlaps with ZoomInfo's data foundation, but PitchBook is private-company-financials-first (~11.9M companies), while ZoomInfo is GTM-contact-first (100M companies, 500M contacts, verified emails / phones). TrustRadius reviewers explicitly describe running both side-by-side: "We use Pitchbooks concurrently with HG and ZoomInfo Sales. Pitchbooks primarily brings the funding status and information to the table." (source: https://www.trustradius.com/compare-products/pitchbook-vs-zoominfo-sales). (2) Legacy ZoomInfo Sales / GTM Workspace prospecting: PitchBook explicitly markets a Business Development use case ("Source high-value prospects, close more deals, and expand into new markets") and a CRM Integration that pushes private-company / deal data into Salesforce, Dynamics, and HubSpot, overlapping with how ZoomInfo Sales / Workspace surfaces company and contact data inside the CRM (source: https://pitchbook.com/products/crm-integration).
- **Adjacent overlap:** ZoomInfo Operations vs PitchBook Direct Data + CRM Integration — both enrich CRM records with company data, but PitchBook is optimized for investor / corp-dev use cases rather than B2B sales hygiene at scale (source: https://pitchbook.com/products). GTM Studio audience-building vs PitchBook screening / Market Maps — both let users build segmented universes, but PitchBook's are sectors / deal types / fund strategies rather than ICP-driven GTM audiences (source: https://pitchbook.com/products).
- **No overlap:** Categorically separate surface area on both sides. PitchBook-only: fund returns benchmarking (IRR / TVPI / DPI), LP commitments and mandates, fundraising pipeline, deal sourcing for PE / VC / M&A, credit underwriting and covenant monitoring, Lumonic portfolio monitoring (sources: https://pitchbook.com/use-cases, https://pitchbook.com/use-cases/deal-sourcing, https://pitchbook.com/products/lumonic). ZoomInfo-only: 200M+ verified emails, 135M+ verified phones, 120M direct dials, intent and technographics, GTM Context Graph, GTM Workspace, GTM Studio, Chorus conversation intelligence, ZoomInfo Marketing / ABM, APIs & MCP and the agent ecosystem. TrustRadius reviewer: "ZoomInfo is far more accurate when producing emails" (source: https://www.trustradius.com/compare-products/pitchbook-vs-zoominfo-sales).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ Third-party comparison on TrustRadius positions PitchBook (Score 8.7/10) as "a resource for data, research, and insights spanning the global capital markets" against ZoomInfo Sales (8.2/10) as "a modern go-to-market platform for B2B companies" — i.e. the two products are framed as serving different categories rather than as direct alternatives (source: https://www.trustradius.com/compare-products/pitchbook-vs-zoominfo-sales).

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "Empowering the evolving private market ecosystem" and "If it's out there, it's in PitchBook" — unrivaled data, research, and technology for private capital markets.
- **Recurring proof points:** Trusted-by logos for BBVA, Fenwick, Kayne Anderson, Lightspeed, MVP, Orrick, Pinterest, TA Associates; the headline data scale (11.9M companies / 3M deals / 621k investors / 161k funds); Morningstar parentage; G2 and TrustRadius 2026 badges; PitchBook-NVCA Venture Monitor and US PE Breakdown research authority.
- **Tone / category framing:** Positioned as a private-capital-markets data, research, and technology platform — explicitly NOT a B2B sales / GTM tool. PitchBook frames itself for investors, dealmakers, and allocators.

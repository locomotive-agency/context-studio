# Unify GTM

## Sub-products

- [[unify-gtm--signal-platform|Unify GTM Signal Platform]] — competes with GTM Workspace
- [[unify-gtm--plays|Unify GTM Plays]] — competes with GTM Workspace

## Summary

Unify GTM is a signal-based outbound platform — bundling 25+ intent signals, waterfall-aggregated B2B contact data, AI research/personalization agents, and managed-deliverability sequencing into one workflow — sold to Sales, Growth, Marketing, and RevOps leaders at high-growth scale-ups (Perplexity, Cursor, Together AI, Hightouch). It overlaps ZoomInfo's Data foundation, GTM Workspace seller surface, and GTM Studio marketer surface most directly, but its data layer is third-party-aggregated (Clearbit, 6sense, Demandbase, Snitcher per their own footer) rather than proprietary. It does not host a first-party Unify-vs-ZoomInfo comparison page, so its anti-vendor argument shows up only in pricing-FAQ form. Pricing is partially public — Growth tier from $1,000–$1,740/mo (depending on plan family), Pro and Enterprise gated as "Custom — annual commitment".

## Product Offerings

### Signals (Intent Data)

- **What it does:** Aggregates 25+ intent signals — website intent (person and company), AI Infinity Signals, champion tracking, new hires, social signals — into outbound triggers.
- **Key features:**
  - 25+ intent signals in one platform (per homepage navigation)
  - AI Infinity Signals
  - Website Traffic intent (person and company resolution)
  - Champion Tracking (track champion job changes)
  - Built-in third-party feeds: 6sense, Clearbit, G2, Bombora-style email intent, job changes, social signals, Lookalike Signals
  - Real-time signal-to-trigger conversion via Plays
- **Source URL:** https://www.unifygtm.com/

### B2B Contact Data

- **What it does:** Built-in waterfall enrichment, prospecting, and qualification layer — pulls verified emails and phone numbers from 30+ sources without requiring users to manage API keys.
- **Key features:**
  - Waterfall data enrichment across 30+ verified sources
  - Persona-based prospecting (rank-order buyer personas)
  - Email enrichment and phone number enrichment
  - Location-based prospecting
  - Automated prospecting workflows triggered by intent
  - Unify Browser Extension to source/enrich from web and CRM
  - Charged only on prospects successfully returned (no charge on misses)
- **Source URL:** https://www.unifygtm.com/product/buyer-data

### Engagement (Sequences + Plays)

- **What it does:** Multi-channel sequencing with managed email deliverability, integrated with Plays — Unify's workflow builder for orchestrating prospecting, AI research, persona-based enrollment, and CRM sync.
- **Key features:**
  - Multi-channel sequencing (email + call sequence steps + manual tasks)
  - Plays workflow builder (intent-triggered automated workflows)
  - Unify Managed Gmail Mailboxes with built-in warmup, bounce checks, domain rotation
  - Real-time email validation
  - Round-robin routing and Slack alerts
  - Reply-in-app unified inbox
  - Manual steps mixed with AI-automated steps
- **Source URL:** https://www.unifygtm.com/products/prospecting

### AI Agents

- **What it does:** Autonomous research and personalization agents that scrape sites, browse the web, read news, and combine with CRM data to qualify accounts and write personalized messaging at scale.
- **Key features:**
  - Custom account/contact research agents (web scraping, news, CRM)
  - Observation Model that constantly gathers context relevant to the business
  - Smart Snippet personalization for human-like outbound copy
  - Transparent reasoning trace (visibility into how agents think)
  - Pre-built questions: fundraise, persona-fit, intent-tool usage, B2B fit, past engagement
  - Powered by OpenAI (per pricing-page logo row)
- **Source URL:** https://www.unifygtm.com/ai

### Reporting & Analytics

- **What it does:** Play-specific dashboards and pipeline-generation reporting with leading and lagging indicators out-of-the-box.
- **Key features:**
  - Play-specific dashboards
  - Pipeline generation tracking with attribution
  - Leading and lagging indicators out-of-the-box
- **Source URL:** https://www.unifygtm.com/

## Target Audience & ICP

- **Industries called out:** B2B SaaS / AI, PLG-led software, financial services (Justworks, SoFi as named customers).
- **Company size called out:** High-growth scale-ups and mid-market; sales orgs without large SDR teams (Guru: "$3M in closed won without an SDR team").
- **Persona / role focus:** Sales (BDRs, AEs, sales reps), Growth marketers, Demand-gen / marketing leaders, RevOps, founder-led GTM teams.
- **Use cases promoted:**
  - Warm outbound (signal-based outreach to prospects who already know the brand)
  - Inbound enrichment / PLG sign-up to enterprise pipeline conversion
  - ABM and account-based orchestration
  - Always-on automated outbound with managed deliverability
  - Persona-based prospecting with AI-personalized sequences
  - Champion tracking on job changes for expansion / new-logo plays

## Integrations & Ecosystem

- **CRMs:** Salesforce, HubSpot CRM, HubSpot Sales Hub.
- **Sales engagement / outreach:** Salesloft, Gmail (native managed-mailbox sending), Slack (alerts).
- **Data / enrichment:** 6sense, Clearbit, G2, Snowflake.
- **Other notable integrations:** Segment, PandaDoc, Bloomreach, Talend Data Catalog, Squarespace, Tradeshift.

Unify's pricing page surfaces 6sense, Clearbit, G2 logos as data partners and shows Salesforce + HubSpot at every tier. Footer of every page reads "Powered by Clearbit, 6sense, Demandbase, and Snitcher" — confirming Unify aggregates third-party data layers rather than building proprietary B2B contact data from scratch.

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Signal-based "warm outbound" framing — pitches itself as the alternative to cold outbound by acting only on intent (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/solutions/marketing).
- 10+ (Slashdot description) / 25+ (homepage) intent signal sources combined in one platform — Slashdot: "more than ten sources of intent data, combined with AI and automation" (source: https://slashdot.org/software/comparison/Unify-vs-ZoomInfo-GTM-Studio/).
- Waterfall enrichment with no API-key management — stitches multiple data vendors so users get better coverage than buying from one vendor alone (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/product/buyer-data).
- Managed Gmail mailboxes with built-in warmup, bounce checks, and domain rotation — deliverability is in the platform, not bolted on (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/pricing).
- Transparent AI Agents — explicitly markets "follow how Agents gather information so outcomes aren't a black box" (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/ai).
- Plays workflow builder — orchestration layer that ties Signals → Prospecting → Enrichment → AI Personalization → Sequencing → CRM in one always-on flow (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

This is the load-bearing section. Compare the competitor against the brand's positioning in `CONTEXT.md`.

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Data foundation vs Unify B2B Contact Data + Signals** — Unify aggregates 25+ intent signals and waterfall-enriches contacts from 30+ verified sources (including 6sense and Clearbit). It directly competes for the "verified emails, phones, intent" RFP slot ZoomInfo's data foundation owns. Unify's pricing-page FAQ argues against single-vendor contact data: "we often have better data than buying data from any one vendor alone" (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/product/buyer-data).
  - **GTM Workspace vs Unify for Sales Reps + AI Agents + Tasks** — Unify Sales pitches "a centralized workspace for all outbound tasks" with always-on agents synthesizing CRM history, news, and web research into per-account reports embedded in the rep workflow — same job ZoomInfo GTM Workspace claims (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/solutions/sales).
  - **GTM Studio vs Unify Marketing + Plays + RevOps solutions** — Unify Marketing pitches "automated outbound on high intent leads in one end-to-end platform … run always-on Plays" — overlaps GTM Studio's natural-language audience building and play orchestration for marketing/RevOps audiences (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/solutions/marketing).
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **GTM Context Graph vs Unify Observation Model + 25+ signals** — Unify pitches an "Observation Model [that] constantly gathers context that is hyper relevant to your business" across web, CRM history, news, and intent — a similar reasoning-across-signals frame as the GTM Context Graph, but built on aggregated third-party feeds rather than 20-year proprietary data plus Chorus/Workbounce-class first-party context capture (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/ai).
  - **ZoomInfo Sales (legacy prospecting UI) vs Unify Prospecting + Browser Extension** — Unify's Browser Extension lets sellers "source and enrich directly from web, CRM, and more" — a direct adjacent substitute for the ZoomInfo Sales Chrome extension and prospecting workflows (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/product/buyer-data).
- **No overlap:**
  - **Conversation intelligence** — no Chorus-equivalent call/meeting recording or coaching product on any reviewed Unify page (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/).
  - **APIs / MCP / agent-builder ecosystem** — Unify exposes APIs (Slashdot: "Has API") but does not market a Claude/GPT MCP listing or developer-facing API surface comparable to ZoomInfo's Enterprise API + mcp.zoominfo.com (source: https://slashdot.org/software/comparison/Unify-vs-ZoomInfo-GTM-Studio/).
  - **Phone / dialer / direct-dial routing** — Unify enriches phone numbers (4 credits each) and supports call sequence steps but does not market a native dialer; ZoomInfo data foundation includes 135M+ phone numbers and 120M direct dials with downstream calling workflows (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/products/prospecting).
  - **Operations: CRM data quality, routing, dedupe** — Unify markets RevOps as a buyer persona but does not surface a ZoomInfo Operations / RingLead-equivalent product line for CRM hygiene, dedupe, and routing on its reviewed pages (source: https://www.unifygtm.com/).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** No first-party Unify-vs-ZoomInfo comparison page exists (Unify hosts only `/comparison/outreach` and `/comparison/artisan`). The closest first-party anti-vendor argument is from Unify's pricing-page FAQ: _"Since Unify aggregates multiple data vendors, not only do we get more data coverage, but we often have better data than buying data from any one vendor alone. Additionally, since our sequencing product has managed deliverability, we can ensure your emails land in the proper inbox, and prevent emails from bouncing."_ The third-party Slashdot comparison summarizes Unify as: "Drive a pipeline utilizing more than ten sources of intent data, combined with AI and automation, to enhance prospecting, personalization, and engagement within a single cohesive workflow" (source: https://slashdot.org/software/comparison/Unify-vs-ZoomInfo-GTM-Studio/).

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "The future of outbound … Intent data and outbound action were meant to be together. Unify brings sales, marketing, and AI together under one roof to deliver pipeline that actually converts." Positioned as "the only solution that brings together intent data and outbound action under one roof" — an outbound "system of action".
- **Recurring proof points:** Justworks 6.8X ROI in 5 months; Spellbook $2.59M pipeline + $250K revenue in 7 months; Juicebox PLG → $3M enterprise pipeline; Perplexity $1.7M pipeline in first 3 months; Pylon 4.2X ROI; flagship logos include Perplexity, Cursor, Together AI, Hightouch, SoFi, Guru, Justworks, OpenPhone, Listen Labs, Navattic.

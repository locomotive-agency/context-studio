# Alta AI

## Sub-products

- [[alta-ai--katie-outbound|Katie — AI SDR Agent]] — competes with GTM Workspace

## Summary

Alta AI is an "AI GTM System of Actions" platform targeting RevOps, sales, marketing, and executive buyers across SMB through enterprise — a coordinated three-agent stack (Katie outbound SDR, Alex inbound calling agent, Luna RevOps growth agent) that runs prospecting, qualification, and revenue intelligence autonomously on top of the customer's CRM. Direct overlap with ZoomInfo concentrates on the GTM Workspace seller surface and the GTM Studio orchestration surface; the most material crossover observation is that Alta is a **consumer** of ZoomInfo's data layer, not a substitute — Alta's homepage and AI SDR pages explicitly list ZoomInfo (alongside Apollo and UpLead) as the prospecting data source, and ZoomInfo appears as a native integration on Alta's `/integrations` page. Alta has no proprietary B2B contact dataset, no developer/MCP surface, and no public Customer Success ICP, all areas where ZoomInfo retains structural advantage.

## Product Offerings

### Katie — AI SDR Agent

- **What it does:** Autonomous outbound AI SDR that identifies ICP prospects, runs multi-channel outreach (email, LinkedIn, calls, SMS), and books meetings.
- **Key features:**
  - Analyzes CRM and 50+ data sources to identify ICP and high-value prospects
  - Multi-channel outreach across email, LinkedIn, and text/SMS
  - AI-personalized messaging trained on customer's sales playbooks
  - Automated 24/7 meeting scheduling
  - Real-time targeting adjustment based on engagement signals
  - References Apollo, ZoomInfo, Salesloft, Warmly, Dripify as ecosystem data/engagement layers
- **Source URL:** https://www.altahq.com/ai-sdr

### Alex — AI Inbound / Calling Agent

- **What it does:** Voice-and-multichannel inbound qualification agent that responds to leads 24/7, qualifies against custom criteria, books meetings, and updates CRM in real time.
- **Key features:**
  - Multi-channel inbound: voice, chat, email, SMS, WhatsApp, LinkedIn
  - 20+ language support
  - Configurable qualification flows trained on customer playbook
  - Real-time CRM and calendar sync
  - Auto-books meetings and sends reminders
  - Conversation analysis feeds outbound (Katie) for compounding learning
- **Source URL:** https://www.altahq.com/ai-inbound-agent

### Luna — AI Growth / RevOps Agent

- **What it does:** Intelligence layer that connects CRM, marketing tools, and 50+ data sources to detect buying signals, build lookalikes, surface recommendations, and orchestrate Katie and Alex.
- **Key features:**
  - Connects 50+ data sources including CRM, intent data, job postings, news, engagement
  - Pattern detection across messaging, channels, and timing
  - Lookalike audience building
  - Surfaces campaign-scale-or-pause recommendations to Slack, email, or Teams
  - Native HubSpot and Salesforce integration
  - Self-optimization via A/B testing across agents
- **Source URL:** https://www.altahq.com/ai

### Revenue Intelligence Platform

- **What it does:** Centralized analytics layer that unifies CRM, calls, emails, and deal engagement into one dashboard for forecasting, pipeline analysis, and revenue management.
- **Key features:**
  - Unified analytics across CRM, calls, emails, and deal engagement
  - Predictive forecasting (claims up to 30% accuracy improvement)
  - AI-powered alerts on data changes
  - Drill-down from executive view to granular details
  - Centralized data management (claims 25% sales-efficiency lift)
  - Targeted at executives, board members, and RevOps
- **Source URL:** https://www.altahq.com/revenue-intelligence-platform

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- "AI GTM System of Actions" framing — coordinated multi-agent system (Katie outbound + Alex inbound + Luna intelligence) where every agent shares context, marketed against single-purpose AI SDR tools (source: https://www.altahq.com/)
- Inbound voice agent (Alex) handles voice/chat/email/SMS/WhatsApp/LinkedIn in 20+ languages — uncommon depth of channel coverage for the AI-SDR category (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai-inbound-agent)
- Sits "alongside your CRM as the layer that decides what to do next and executes it automatically" — framed as a System of Actions on top of the CRM's System of Record (source: https://www.altahq.com/revenue-intelligence-platform)
- Luna self-optimizes via A/B testing across agents and claims compounding gains: "72% faster lead response, 3× qualified meetings, 21h saved/week" (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai)
- Native Salesforce AppExchange listing with bidirectional sync, custom-object support, dedup, OAuth, and real-time push (source: https://www.altahq.com/integrations/salesforce)
- SOC2 and ISO 27001 compliance referenced repeatedly as enterprise readiness signal (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai-inbound-agent)

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:** Alta's Katie (AI SDR) overlaps **GTM Workspace** — both surfaces prioritize accounts and run AI-drafted outreach for sellers, but Katie runs autonomously where GTM Workspace's AI agent layer is a co-pilot (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai-sdr). Luna (AI Growth) overlaps **GTM Studio** in the play-orchestration / lookalike-building / signal-routing job-to-be-done (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai). Alta's Revenue Intelligence Platform + Salesforce sync touches **ZoomInfo Sales** (legacy prospecting UI) and **ZoomInfo Operations** (CRM data quality + routing) (source: https://www.altahq.com/revenue-intelligence-platform).
- **Adjacent overlap:** Luna's "compounding intelligence" framing is conceptually adjacent to ZoomInfo's **GTM Context Graph**, but Alta does not bring proprietary third-party B2B data depth — it unifies the customer's first-party CRM plus enrichment-vendor inputs (including ZoomInfo) into a signal layer (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai). Alex's conversation analysis loop touches **Chorus's** turf in coaching/intelligence, though Chorus is also explicitly listed as a native Alta integration (source: https://www.altahq.com/integrations).
- **No overlap:**
  - **Proprietary B2B contact data foundation** — Alta has no first-party dataset; the AI SDR page lists "Apollo, Zoominfo" as the data layer Katie reads, and ZoomInfo + UpLead appear under "Data source" on /integrations. Alta is a **consumer** of B2B data, not a producer (source: https://www.altahq.com/integrations).
  - **Developer / agent-builder ecosystem** — no public Enterprise API, no MCP server, no Claude/agent-directory presence referenced on any reviewed page. Alta's only surface is the agent UI plus integrations (source: https://www.altahq.com/integrations).
  - **Autonomous voice / calling agent (Alex)** — ZoomInfo's GTM stack does not include a 20+-language autonomous outbound or inbound voice agent at equivalent scope; this is competitor-only space (source: https://www.altahq.com/ai-inbound-agent).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ A third-party Slashdot comparison page exists at https://slashdot.org/software/comparison/Alta-AI-Sales-vs-ZoomInfo-GTM-Studio/ but it was Cloudflare-blocked across all 3 fallback extractor tiers (requests:403; curl_cffi:403; stealth:cloudflare). ## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "AI GTM System of Actions" — coordinated AI agents (Katie/Alex/Luna) that automate prospecting, inbound qualification, and revenue intelligence so "AI acts, your team wins." Headline customer claim: "3× more qualified meetings, 20+ hours saved per rep per week."
- **Recurring proof points:** monday.com (outbound meetings 120 → 180/mo, 14h saved per Enterprise AE/week); Riverside (Alta handles 30% of inbound, replaced one SDR); GForce Life Sciences (signal-based outbound at scale); TL Divisione Tessile (10% reply rate on Italian-localized outbound); CloudKitchens / Mesh / PayPal testimonials; "approaching 100 paying customers including Fortune 500 and SMBs" (Business Insider, March 2025).
- **Tone / category framing:** Calls itself the "AI GTM System of Actions" and an "AI Revenue Workforce." Explicitly contrasts itself with "point solutions" and "fragmented systems." Frames the CRM as the System of Record and Alta as the System of Actions that sits on top of it.

## Summary

Lavender is an AI email-coaching tool — narrowly scoped to sales-email writing assistance ("Master the Art of Sales Emails") rather than a broad B2B GTM platform. The product surface is two products: **The Email Coach** (real-time coaching as reps write emails, available free) and **Ora** (an autonomous AI sales agent that researches, writes, and sends personalized sales emails). Lavender is best understood as a **specialized AI-augmentation layer for cold email writing**, with very narrow direct competitive overlap with ZoomInfo. The strongest crossover is with ZoomInfo's AI email writer / GTM Workspace AI features, but Lavender does not compete on contact data, intent, conversation intelligence, ABM, or any other ZoomInfo pillar. Pricing not surfaced in dollar amounts on the extracted pages — multiple tier references but no public $ values.

## Product Offerings

### The Email Coach

- **What it does:** Real-time AI email-writing coaching — analyzes draft emails as reps write them and suggests improvements for response rates.
- **Key features:**
  - Real-time email writing coaching
  - Subject-line + body optimization
  - Reply-rate prediction
  - Browser extension for in-Gmail / in-Outlook coaching
  - Free tier ("Install the Coach for Free")
  - "For Sellers / For Leaders / For Anyone" persona segments
- **Source URL:** https://www.lavender.ai/

### Ora (AI Sales Agent)

- **What it does:** Autonomous AI sales agent that researches prospects, writes personalized emails, and sends them automatically — Lavender's expansion beyond coaching into agent-driven outbound.
- **Key features:**
  - "Designed to work like a top rep"
  - Upload lead list + train the agent on rep style
  - Reads existing inbox to learn rep voice
  - Natural-language instructions ("Talk to it like you would a rep")
  - CRM enrichment integration
  - Autonomous send capability
- **Source URL:** https://www.lavender.ai/ora

### CRM Enrichment

- **What it does:** Enriches CRM records with prospect context to fuel Ora's email writing.
- **Key features:**
  - CRM record enrichment
  - Powers Ora's personalization
  - Integrates into Ora workflow
- **Source URL:** https://www.lavender.ai/crm (referenced)

### LevelUp / Sales Certification

- **What it does:** Educational platform — Email Wizard certification courses, sales-email training content (LavenderLand + The Lavender Letter newsletter).
- **Key features:**
  - Email Wizard certification (lavender.thinkific.com/courses/EmailWizard101)
  - The Lavender Letter newsletter
  - LavenderLand brand content
  - SDR onboarding and training playbooks
- **Source URL:** https://levelup.lavender.ai/

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Narrow scope (email-writing coaching) vs. broad GTM platform — Lavender deliberately does not compete on data or workflow breadth (source: https://www.lavender.ai/).
- Free Email Coach as the primary acquisition surface — install the coach in Gmail/Outlook for free (source: https://www.lavender.ai/).
- Ora as an autonomous AI sales agent — Lavender's expansion into agent-driven outbound, training the agent on the rep's actual inbox style (source: https://www.lavender.ai/ora).
- Email Wizard Certification + LavenderLand brand — strong content + community flywheel for SDR education (source: https://levelup.lavender.ai/).
- "For Anyone" tier — Lavender markets to individual sellers, not just enterprise teams.
- Personality-forward brand voice ("Your Magical AI Email Coach", "Email Wizard") — distinctive in the otherwise-corporate B2B sales-tech space.

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo's AI email writer (inside GTM Workspace) ↔ Lavender Email Coach + Ora.** Both vendors offer AI email writing assistance. Lavender's Email Coach is real-time in-line coaching; Ora is autonomous agent-driven send. ZoomInfo's AI email writer is data-aware (uses ZoomInfo contact data + intent signals); Lavender's Ora trains on the rep's actual inbox. (source: https://www.lavender.ai/ora)
  - **ZoomInfo's "team of AI agents" framing ↔ Lavender Ora as a sales agent.** Narrow head-to-head on the autonomous-sales-agent surface. Ora is more focused on email-only autonomy; ZoomInfo's agents span broader workflows. (source: https://www.lavender.ai/ora)
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Sales / GTM Workspace seller surface ↔ Lavender Email Coach as a Gmail/Outlook overlay.** Both deliver in-workflow AI assistance to sellers but at very different scope.
- **No overlap:**
  - **B2B contact data (Pillar 1)** — Lavender has no contact database.
  - **ZoomInfo Chorus / Conversation Intelligence** — no CI product.
  - **ZoomInfo Marketing / FormComplete / WebSights / ABM platform** — Lavender has no marketing-side product.
  - **Forecasting / pipeline management** — not in scope.
  - **CRM enrichment at the broad level** — Lavender has CRM Enrichment but only for Ora's purposes, not as a standalone product.
  - **MCP / agent-data lane at the data layer** — Lavender doesn't sit in the agent-data lane (Ora is the agent; the data is rep's own inbox).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public Lavender-authored vs-ZoomInfo page surfaced._ The competitive relationship is narrow — Lavender competes on email-writing AI quality, not on data or platform scope. Many ZoomInfo customers use Lavender alongside ZoomInfo (data from ZoomInfo + writing coach from Lavender).

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "Lavender — Your Magical AI Email Coach. Master the Art of Sales Emails."
- **Recurring proof points:** Email Wizard Certification; LavenderLand brand content; "Designed to work like a top rep" (Ora framing); "Lavender's read your inbox to learn what works."
- **Tone / category framing:** Personality-forward, education-heavy ("LavenderLand," "Email Wizard," "The Lavender Letter") — Lavender deliberately stays out of the corporate B2B-platform race and instead positions as a specialized AI augmentation tool for individual sellers + SDR teams. The Ora launch shifts Lavender into the autonomous AI sales agent lane, where the competitive set widens to include 11x and Artisan rather than ZoomInfo directly.

## Summary

Salesloft is "The Leading AI Revenue Orchestration Platform," with 4,000+ customers — the closest direct competitor to ZoomInfo's GTM Workspace seller surface, plus a conversation-intelligence layer that competes with Chorus. Salesloft's 2026 pivot is into "Revenue Orchestration" — combining Cadence (sequencing), Conversations (CI), Deals (forecasting), AI agents, and an Enterprise Data Platform underneath. Salesloft does not own a B2B contact database and has no equivalent to ZoomInfo's Pillar 1 (Data) or ABM platform — instead it positions an Enterprise Data Platform that acts as a unified revenue model on top of customer + integrated data. Pricing is two-tier (Advanced and Elite), quote-based — no public dollar amounts. AI Agents (Account Agents, Conversation Agents) are the most aggressive new frontier, packaged similarly to HubSpot Breeze and Gong's "team of AI agents" framing.

## Product Offerings

### Salesloft Revenue Orchestration Platform (umbrella)

- **What it does:** Unified platform for revenue teams to "Create. Convert. Close." — orchestrates engagement, conversation intelligence, deal management, and AI agents on top of an Enterprise Data Platform.
- **Key features:**
  - Sales engagement / Cadence sequencing
  - Conversation intelligence
  - Deal management & forecasting
  - AI agents (Account, Conversation)
  - Enterprise Data Platform underneath
  - "Where revenue teams go to" (Analyze, Chat, etc.)
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/

### Cadence (Sales Engagement)

- **What it does:** Sequencing platform for outbound and follow-up motions — dialer, email, social, and task workflows in one cadence.
- **Key features:**
  - Multi-channel cadences (email, dialer, social, task)
  - Built-in dialer
  - Cadence templates and optimization
  - A/B testing on cadence steps
  - Native integration with CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot, MS Dynamics)
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/ (referenced; specific Cadence page not in this run)

### Conversations (Conversation Intelligence)

- **What it does:** Conversation intelligence software that analyzes calls and meetings to surface deal-level insights, coaching opportunities, and seller-action triggers.
- **Key features:**
  - Call recording and transcription
  - AI-powered conversation analytics
  - Deal-momentum signals
  - Coaching dashboards / coachable moments
  - Insights into "what's happening in every deal"
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/platform/conversations

### Conversation Agents (AI Agents — Conversations)

- **What it does:** AI agents that automate post-call busywork — generate summaries, surface insights, and trigger productive actions.
- **Key features:**
  - Automate the busywork (note-taking, summaries)
  - Intelligent insights from every conversation
  - Drive more productive conversations
  - Layered on top of Conversations CI
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/platform/conversations/conversation-agents

### AI Agents (platform-wide)

- **What it does:** "The missing piece of productivity" — agents that know what sellers need before they do, working across the platform.
- **Key features:**
  - Agents proactive, not reactive
  - Cross-functional across Cadence + Conversations + Deals
  - Account Agents (separate add-on, automates account research)
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/platform/ai-agents

### Enterprise Data Platform

- **What it does:** Salesloft's data foundation for Revenue Orchestration — combines agentic AI, an enterprise data model, and a unified revenue platform.
- **Key features:**
  - Agentic AI infrastructure
  - Enterprise data model (unified revenue object schema)
  - Unified revenue platform layer
  - Real revenue-outcome focus
  - Replaces the patchwork of disconnected sales tools
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/platform/enterprise-data-platform

### Deals (Deal Management & Forecasting)

- **What it does:** Pipeline and deal management with forecasting (referenced via "Salesloft Packages and Availability" on the help page).
- **Key features:**
  - Pipeline boards
  - Deal-progression analytics
  - Forecast roll-ups
  - Deal-risk surfacing
- **Source URL:** https://www.salesloft.com/ (referenced)

## Target Audience & ICP

- **Industries called out:** Cross-industry; case-study customer Fullsteam (payments / commerce) is featured; Emerson is also referenced. 4,000+ customers total.
- **Company size called out:** Mid-Market through Enterprise — Enterprise Revenue Orchestration is positioned as the Tier 1 use case.
- **Persona / role focus:** Sales Leaders, AEs, SDRs, RevOps, Sales Enablement / Coaching.
- **Use cases promoted:** Pipeline creation and coverage; team productivity and performance; technology and workflow optimization; AI-augmented seller workflows; deal forecasting; coaching at scale.

## Integrations & Ecosystem

- **CRMs:** Salesforce (deep), HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics — native CRM integrations.
- **Sales engagement / outreach:** Salesloft IS the engagement product — competes head-on with Outreach.
- **Data / enrichment:** No native contact database — relies on partners (ZoomInfo, Apollo, Clearbit, etc.) for data into Cadence.
- **Other notable integrations:** LinkedIn (Sales Navigator), Slack, calendar / email (Google, Microsoft 365), video tools, conversation-intelligence integrations. Marketplace via the platform.

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- "Revenue Orchestration" category framing — Salesloft is the most aggressive vendor on this category in 2026, positioning above pure sales engagement (source: https://www.salesloft.com/platform/enterprise-revenue-orchestration).
- 4,000+ customer base — established mid-market through enterprise footprint (source: https://www.salesloft.com/).
- AI Agents framework — Conversation Agents (live) + Account Agents (add-on) + platform-wide AI agents — packaged similarly to HubSpot Breeze and Gong's specialized agents (source: https://www.salesloft.com/platform/ai-agents).
- Enterprise Data Platform underneath — agentic AI + enterprise data model + unified revenue platform (source: https://www.salesloft.com/platform/enterprise-data-platform).
- Deep CRM integrations as a coexistence model — Salesloft sits on top of CRM and data, doesn't try to replace either.

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Sales / GTM Workspace ↔ Salesloft Cadence + Deals + AI Agents.** Direct head-to-head on the seller workspace surface — both pitch unified seller experience. Salesloft's strength is sequencing depth and Conversation Agents; ZoomInfo's strength is the data foundation underneath. Many customers run BOTH. (source: https://www.salesloft.com/)
  - **ZoomInfo Chorus ↔ Salesloft Conversations + Conversation Agents.** Direct CI competitor. Salesloft's CI is bundled into the platform; Chorus is sold as a standalone or bundled with ZoomInfo Sales. (source: https://www.salesloft.com/platform/conversations)
  - **ZoomInfo GTM Context Graph (Pillar 2 reasoning) ↔ Salesloft Enterprise Data Platform + Agentic AI.** Both pitch a unified data model that AI agents reason on. Salesloft's frame is "revenue orchestration"; ZoomInfo's is "GTM intelligence." (source: https://www.salesloft.com/platform/enterprise-data-platform)
  - **ZoomInfo's "team of AI agents" framing ↔ Salesloft's Conversation Agents + Account Agents.** Both vendors landed on the same "specialized AI agents" packaging in the same year.
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo APIs & MCP ↔ Salesloft API.** Salesloft has an integration ecosystem but no documented MCP server.
- **No overlap:**
  - **B2B contact database (Pillar 1).** Salesloft has none — relies on partners.
  - **ABM Platform.** Salesloft is not a Gartner MQ ABM Platforms competitor.
  - **Marketing Hub / WebSights / FormComplete.** No marketing-side product.
  - **Standalone enrichment / Operations.** No equivalent.
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public Salesloft-authored vs-ZoomInfo page surfaced._ Salesloft's competitive framing is positional ("Revenue Orchestration" as a category above sales engagement) rather than naming ZoomInfo specifically. Salesloft typically competes most directly with Outreach.

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "The Leading AI Revenue Orchestration Platform." Create. Convert. Close. Trusted by 4,000+ customers.
- **Recurring proof points:** 4,000+ customers; Fullsteam case study (sales acceleration); Emerson case study; "Revenue Orchestration" thought-leadership.
- **Tone / category framing:** Salesloft owns "Revenue Orchestration" as a category — explicitly positioned as the next evolution beyond sales engagement (which it pioneered). The framing puts Salesloft in competition with Gong's "Revenue AI OS" message and adjacent to ZoomInfo's "All-in-one AI GTM Platform" — three vendors converging on the same operating-system framing from three different starting points (engagement, CI, and data respectively).

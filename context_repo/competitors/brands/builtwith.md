## Summary

BuiltWith is a web-technology profiling and technographic data vendor that sells exportable lead lists, sales intelligence, and CRM enrichment to sales, marketing, and investor/analyst buyers. It overlaps directly with the technographic slice of ZoomInfo's Data foundation — claiming 113,760+ tracked technologies across 414M domains with 41 years of usage history — and lightly with ZoomInfo's intent layer via "Actionable Insights" that fires when target accounts trial, experiment with, or rebuild their stacks. It does not compete with ZoomInfo's GTM Workspace, GTM Studio, or full firmographic/contact graph; coverage of contact emails is limited to non-EU/CA records bundled with technographic exports. Pricing is fully public and self-service from a free tier through $295–$995/month, plus PAYG API credits, Firehose, and Datasets up to $187K.

## Product Offerings

### BuiltWith Pro (Lead Generation / Technology Lists)

> **Wiki:** [[builtwith--pro-lead-generation]]

- **What it does:** Builds exportable lead lists of websites filtered by the web technologies they run, with attributes like spend, revenue, location, employee count, and contacts.
- **Key features:**
  - 113,760+ tracked web technologies across 414M domains, with usage history back to 1985
  - Technology, keyword, eCommerce/retail, and SKU-based list creation
  - Filter by usage duration, technology spend, traffic rank, verticals (IAB), financials, location
  - Contact names, titles, and emails for non-EU/CA companies (GDPR/CCPA-restricted)
  - Excel/CSV export with no record cap inside the export
  - Predictive Leads / Future Customers based on stack-similarity
- **Source URL:** https://builtwith.com/lead-generation

### Sales Intelligence

> **Wiki:** [[builtwith--sales-intelligence]]

- **What it does:** Surfaces a target account's full website tech profile and competitor/churn movement so sellers can have informed conversations and time outreach to tech-change events.
- **Key features:**
  - One-click website Technology Lookup (full stack snapshot)
  - Competitor Comparison reports (market share, churn between technologies, switcher lists)
  - Actionable Insights: real-time signals from trials, experimentation, rebuilds in a target's stack
  - LeadsEye visual lead qualification of uploaded lists
  - Historical technology usage and registered contact info on lookup reports
- **Source URL:** https://builtwith.com/sales-intelligence

### CRM Integrations (Bulk Sync + Lead Push)

> **Wiki:** [[builtwith--crm-integrations]]

- **What it does:** Pushes BuiltWith technology and contact attributes into CRMs and data warehouses, including bulk daily sync and per-record lead push.
- **Key features:**
  - Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics 365, Zoho: Bulk Sync up to 300,000 records/day
  - Salesforce, HubSpot: Lead Push from inside reports
  - Close, Pipedrive (free plugin), Gmail (free), Zendesk (free), Slack net-new alerts
  - Snowflake / Redshift / BigQuery / Databricks data-lake delivery via Datasets
  - Adobe Marketo webhook endpoint, Zapier connector, Crunchbase Data Boost
  - MCP integration for Claude AI and other MCP connectors
- **Source URL:** https://builtwith.com/crm-integrations

### API & Datasets

> **Wiki:** [[builtwith--api-datasets]]

- **What it does:** Programmatic access to BuiltWith technology data via per-domain API credits, bulk Firehose lookups, and downloadable Parquet/CSV datasets.
- **Key features:**
  - API Credits PAYG: $99 for 2,000 credits, no expiry, charged per successful domain return
  - Firehose live tech lookup: $315.45 for 5,000 websites
  - Datasets (entire-internet or per-tech archives): $1,721 to $187,000 based on list size
  - Parquet or flat-CSV delivery for Snowflake / Redshift / BigQuery / Databricks
- **Source URL:** https://builtwith.com/all-products

### Browser Extensions, Trends & BuiltWith Ask

> **Wiki:** [[builtwith--browser-extensions]]

- **What it does:** Free site-by-site profiling tools (Chrome / Firefox / Opera extensions), public web technology trend statistics, and BuiltWith Ask natural-language interface.
- **Key features:**
  - Free individual-site lookups forever
  - Public Technology Trends across 113k+ technologies
  - Top Sites, Public Companies (Pro Financial), Technology Investor Center
  - BuiltWith Ask natural-language query tool
  - Browser extensions for Chrome, Firefox, Opera
- **Source URL:** https://builtwith.com/

- Predictive Leads / Future Customers — surfaces sites whose stack profile predicts adoption of a target technology before they switch (source: https://builtwith.com/lead-generation).
- Native MCP integration for Claude AI and other MCP connectors, plus dedicated API & AI Agents endpoint and dataset delivery to Snowflake / BigQuery / Databricks (source: https://builtwith.com/crm-integrations).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **Data (Pillar 1) — technographics:** BuiltWith's headline product (113,760 web technologies across 414M domains with 41 years of history) is a direct substitute for ZoomInfo's 30K+-technology technographic layer inside Data (Pillar 1) (source: https://builtwith.com/plans).
  - **Data (Pillar 1) — contacts/emails (limited):** BuiltWith ships fully-exportable contact names, titles, and emails for non-EU/CA companies bundled with technographic exports — overlapping ZoomInfo's contact data layer in the technographic-led prospecting use case (source: https://builtwith.com/lead-generation).
  - **Intent Data layer (Data + GTM Context Graph):** "Actionable Insights" claims to detect "companies showing clear signals they are about to buy, upgrade, or switch technologies" from real-time stack movement — competing with the buying-intent slice of ZoomInfo's Data + GTM Context Graph (source: https://builtwith.com/sales-intelligence).
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Operations:** BuiltWith's Bulk Sync / Lead Push pushes up to 300,000 enriched records/day into Salesforce, HubSpot, Dynamics 365, and Zoho — adjacent to ZoomInfo Operations' enrichment role but scoped to technographic attributes only, not full firmographic/contact ops (source: https://builtwith.com/crm-integrations).
  - **APIs & MCP:** BuiltWith offers a PAYG API, dataset delivery to Snowflake/BigQuery/Databricks/Redshift, and a Claude / MCP connector — adjacent to ZoomInfo's APIs & MCP, but limited to web-technology data (source: https://builtwith.com/crm-integrations).
- **No overlap:**
  - Cyber risk auditing of third-party tech stacks (source: https://builtwith.com/).
  - Investor / public-markets alternative data via the Technology Investor Center, Public Companies dashboard, and Alternative Financial Data product line (source: https://builtwith.com/).
  - eCommerce product / SKU intelligence — 2 billion products across 26M+ stores with retail spend and SKU counts (source: https://builtwith.com/).
  - ZoomInfo's GTM Workspace (seller front-end with AI-drafted outreach), GTM Studio (marketer/RevOps natural-language audience builder), Chorus (conversation intelligence), and ZoomInfo Chat have no BuiltWith equivalents.
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ See flag `vs_brand_missing`. Third-party comparison content surfaced (DiscoverCloud, Cuspera, AeroLeads, FindStack, SimilarWeb traffic comparison) but no BuiltWith-authored vs/comparison page was found.

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "Find out what websites are Built With" — turn 41 years of web-technology adoption data into lead lists, market share reports, and tech-switch intent signals (source: https://builtwith.com/).
- **Recurring proof points:** 113,760+ tracked technologies; 414M domains; data back to January 1985; "Join over 1 million people using BuiltWith Pro"; press placements in Financial Times, Winton Capital, Harvard, MIT Technology Review, and WSJ; customer quotes from Carted, Listrak, Pearl Lemon, and Mouseflow; site-count badges (6.8M Shopify, 576K Klaviyo, 480K HubSpot, 255K Zendesk, 155K Salesforce).
- **Tone / category framing:** Web technology profiler / technographics + lead generation; positions itself as the "ultimate competitive advantage" for sales and as the lead source "firmographic data misses."

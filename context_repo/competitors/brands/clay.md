## Summary

Clay is a GTM data orchestration and AI workflow platform that competes with ZoomInfo's Pillar 1 (Data) and Operations enrichment in a fundamentally different shape: instead of being a single source of contact data, Clay aggregates 150+ data sources via "waterfall enrichment" and lets GTM Ops / RevOps teams build custom workflows on top. The most pointed competitive claim Clay publishes lives in its FAQ: "We often double or triple data coverage rates at a 1/5th or less of the cost of Zoominfo... Clay, on the other hand, is very transparent. We tell you exactly which individual providers your data comes from, and we give you access to wholesale rates from them." Clay also ships an MCP server (clay.com/mcp) — a direct echo of ZoomInfo's MCP positioning, and the only Tier 1 ZoomInfo competitor in this batch with a documented MCP integration. Pricing is fully public, tier-based ($0 → $185/mo → $495/mo → custom), with "Data Credits" and "Actions" as the two consumption units. Customer logos include OpenAI, Rippling, Recharge, Anthropic, Canva.

## Product Offerings

### [[clay--platform|Clay Platform (Data Orchestration + Workflows)]]

- **What it does:** Spreadsheet-style canvas where GTM teams orchestrate data from 150+ sources, run AI-powered enrichment, and trigger downstream actions in CRMs, email tools, and ad platforms.
- **Key features:**
  - Orchestrate data from 150+ sources across CRM, enrichment vendors, and signals
  - Spreadsheet/table-based workflow canvas
  - Claygent — Clay's AI agent with web research access for custom data points
  - Clean and format data with AI in seconds
  - Iterable, flexible workflows (no code)
  - Bring your own API key
- **Source URL:** https://www.clay.com/

### [[clay--waterfall-enrichment|Waterfall Enrichment (Multi-provider Enrichment)]]

- **What it does:** Runs requests across multiple data providers in priority order, falling back to next provider when primary returns no match — maximizes coverage on contact info (work emails, personal emails, mobile numbers).
- **Key features:**
  - Find work emails across multiple providers
  - Find personal emails across multiple providers
  - Find mobile numbers across multiple providers
  - Waterfall enrichment for any custom data point
  - Wholesale rates from individual providers (transparent vs. whitelabel markup)
  - 150+ supported providers
- **Source URL:** https://www.clay.com/waterfall-enrichment

### [[clay--inbound-enrichment|Inbound Enrichment]]

- **What it does:** Real-time enrichment of every inbound lead with rich buyer context from 150+ sources, then triggers downstream action.
- **Key features:**
  - Sync every lead source
  - Rich buyer context from 150+ data sources
  - Real-time enrichment + triggered action
  - 3x stat (page proof points: 3x, 5 min, 2x)
  - Forms / webhooks / CRM integration
- **Source URL:** https://www.clay.com/use-cases/inbound-enrichment

### [[clay--account-research|Account Research (Automated)]]

- **What it does:** Puts account research on auto-pilot — generates pre-call notes, account/contact research, and signal detection for sellers.
- **Key features:**
  - Research accounts & contacts at scale
  - Generate pre-call notes
  - Act on the latest signals (job changes, hiring, news)
  - Clay in ChatGPT — run account research in natural language
  - Pre-call brief generation in CRM (Salesforce surface)
- **Source URL:** https://www.clay.com/use-cases/account-research

### [[clay--mcp|Clay MCP Server]]

- **What it does:** Native MCP integration — Clay data accessible inside any MCP-compatible AI assistant.
- **Key features:**
  - Clay data accessible programmatically via MCP
  - Direct AI-agent integration (separate from Claygent)
- **Source URL:** https://www.clay.com/mcp

### Sculptor (Natural Language Workflow Builder)

- **What it does:** Build GTM workflows with natural language (referenced from pricing/integrations pages).
- **Key features:**
  - Natural-language workflow construction
  - Layer on top of the table/spreadsheet canvas
- **Source URL:** https://www.clay.com/sculptor

## Target Audience & ICP

- **Industries called out:** Cross-industry; case studies feature OpenAI (AI), Rippling (HR/Finance), Recharge (e-commerce), with reference to Anthropic and Canva on the integrations page.
- **Company size called out:** SMB (Free + Launch) → Mid-market (Growth) → Enterprise. Dedicated `/enterprise` and `/clay-for-startups` pages.
- **Persona / role focus:** GTM Ops / RevOps (primary), Marketing, Sales — and an explicit "GTM Engineer" category Clay actively cultivates (TheGTME.com job board, university, cohorts).
- **Use cases promoted:** CRM enrichment, Reverse ETL, Account research, Prospecting, Outbound, Rep assist, Automated inbound, PLG assist, Territory planning, ABM, TAM sourcing.

## Integrations & Ecosystem

- **CRMs:** Salesforce, HubSpot — native sync, deep enrichment.
- **Sales engagement / outreach:** Outreach, Salesloft (via integration partners).
- **Data / enrichment:** 150+ data providers in waterfall — Apollo (yes — Apollo is a Clay integration partner), ContactOut, Datagma, Findymail, Hunter, Bouncer, plus dozens of others. Clay positions itself as orchestration LAYER above any vendor.
- **Other notable integrations:** ChatGPT (Clay-in-ChatGPT for natural-language account research), Anthropic Claude, MCP server (Clay MCP), Slack, ad platforms (LinkedIn, Google Ads, Meta), data warehouses (Snowflake, BigQuery via reverse ETL).

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Multi-provider waterfall enrichment — the architectural pitch is "Why use one contact database when you can use them all?" Clay aggregates instead of competing as a primary data source (source: https://www.clay.com/waterfall-enrichment).
- Transparent provider pricing — Clay names the vendors data comes from and exposes wholesale rates, vs. ZoomInfo's whitelabeled / single-vendor-with-markup model (source: https://www.clay.com/faq/how-can-clay-give-me-better-data-coverage-and-pricing-than-zoominfo-or-other-data-providers).
- AI-native workflows — Claygent (AI agent with web research) for custom data points + Sculptor (natural-language workflow builder).
- MCP server (clay.com/mcp) — the only Tier 1 ZoomInfo competitor in this batch with a documented MCP integration analogous to ZoomInfo's mcp.zoominfo.com.
- Public, tier-based pricing with a calculator — prospects can self-size before talking to sales.
- "GTM Engineer" category creation — Clay actively cultivates a persona (TheGTME.com, university, cohorts, partner network) that ZoomInfo's developer story does not.
- OpenAI customer claim: "more than doubled enrichment coverage from low 40% to high 80%" (source: https://www.clay.com/customers/open-ai).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Data foundation (Pillar 1) ↔ Clay Waterfall Enrichment.** Clay aggregates 150+ providers including ZoomInfo competitors (Apollo, Hunter, etc.) and pitches better coverage at lower cost via wholesale-rate access. Clay does NOT have a primary contact database of its own — it is an orchestration layer. (source: https://www.clay.com/waterfall-enrichment, https://www.clay.com/faq/how-can-clay-give-me-better-data-coverage-and-pricing-than-zoominfo-or-other-data-providers)
  - **ZoomInfo Operations / GTM Studio (data ops, enrichment, workflow orchestration) ↔ Clay Platform.** This is the most direct overlap — both target RevOps / GTM Ops teams and orchestrate data into CRM. Clay's spreadsheet-canvas + AI workflow model is a different UX than GTM Studio's natural-language builder, but the use cases (CRM enrichment, audience building, signal-driven plays) are identical. (source: https://www.clay.com/clay-for-gtm-ops)
  - **ZoomInfo APIs & MCP ↔ Clay API + Clay MCP.** Clay ships an MCP server (clay.com/mcp) — direct analogue to ZoomInfo's mcp.zoominfo.com positioning. (source: https://www.clay.com/mcp)
  - **ZoomInfo Marketing FormComplete / inbound workflows ↔ Clay Inbound Enrichment.** Both enrich inbound leads in real time from form fills.
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Sales / GTM Workspace seller workflows ↔ Clay Account Research + Rep Assist.** Clay's Salesforce-native pre-call notes and account research compete with the seller-research surface in GTM Workspace, but Clay does not run sequences or dialer.
- **No overlap:**
  - **Conversation intelligence (Chorus).** No equivalent.
  - **Sales engagement / sequencing / dialer.** Clay integrates with sequencing tools but does not replace them. There is no Outbound surface comparable to GTM Workspace.
  - **Standalone B2B contact database.** Clay deliberately does not own one — that is the architectural pitch.
  - **ABM platform (Gartner MQ).** Not a Clay category.
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** Direct quote from Clay's FAQ: _"We often double or triple data coverage rates at a 1/5th or less of the cost of Zoominfo. A recent enterprise client, seeking to enrich inbound leads with basic company/contact info, had 30% coverage rates with Zoominfo at about ~25 cents per enrichment. They increased their coverage rates to 80% while cutting costs to less than ~1 cent per enrichment with Clay. ... Large data providers like Zoominfo buy and whitelabel small providers, then charging a massive premium for subscriptions. Clay, on the other hand, is very transparent."_ (source: https://www.clay.com/faq/how-can-clay-give-me-better-data-coverage-and-pricing-than-zoominfo-or-other-data-providers)

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "Go to market with unique data — and the ability to act on it." Every GTM data point imaginable, in one place; AI that's contextual, consistent, and scalable; orchestrate and act on data at scale.
- **Recurring proof points:** OpenAI (40% → 80% enrichment coverage), Rippling, Recharge, customers like Anthropic and Canva. "World's best GTM Ops and RevOps teams" framing.
- **Tone / category framing:** "GTM data orchestration" and "GTM Engineering" — Clay is creating a new category around the orchestration model, explicitly positioned against single-source data vendors. The pitch is architectural ("waterfall is fundamentally better than a single source") rather than feature-by-feature.

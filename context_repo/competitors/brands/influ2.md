## Summary

Influ2 is a contact-level (person-based) ABM advertising platform aimed at B2B Marketing, Demand Gen, and RevOps teams that want to run ads against named individuals from their CRM rather than account-level audiences. Its product set covers contact-level ad delivery, contact-level intent, multi-stage buyer journey orchestration, a sales-facing Signals Center, and form-less revenue attribution — all seeded by lists synced from Salesforce or HubSpot. The most material overlap with ZoomInfo is the **ZoomInfo Marketing / ABM advertising and intent** play (GTM Studio audience building + advertising orchestration); Influ2 explicitly competes there on the "person, not account" angle and positions account-level ABM as insufficient. Influ2 does NOT sell the underlying B2B contact/company database, CRM data quality, or any developer/MCP layer that ZoomInfo offers — it assumes the customer already has the data in their CRM.

## Product Offerings

### Contact-Level Ads

> **Wiki:** [[influ2--contact-level-ads]]

- **What it does:** Targets named individuals (synced from CRM) with display and social ads, then attributes ad engagement back to specific people and pipeline (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-ads).
- **Key features:**
  - Choose specific contacts to target by syncing lists directly from CRM
  - Distributes ads across LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Google, Bing, and Amazon
  - Real-time alerts to sales when a targeted contact sees or engages with an ad
  - Per-contact engagement tracking (impressions, clicks, visits) without form fills
  - Revenue attribution tying ad engagement to pipeline created, progressed, and won
- **Source URL:** https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-ads

### Contact-Level Intent

> **Wiki:** [[influ2--contact-level-intent]]

- **What it does:** Surfaces first-party intent signals tied to named individuals across social, search, ads, third-party content, and the customer's website (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-intent).
- **Key features:**
  - Identifies which named buyers are researching specified topics across the web
  - Tracks search intent even in zero-click scenarios
  - Separates buyer intent from social chatter on individual buyers' posts
  - Identifies website visitors at the contact level (Beta)
  - Auto-distributes ads to where each prospect is most active
- **Source URL:** https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-intent

### Custom Buyer Journeys (Buyer Journey Orchestration)

> **Wiki:** [[influ2--custom-buyer-journeys]]

- **What it does:** Lets marketers define multi-stage journeys that automatically swap ad messaging and trigger sales/MAP actions based on each contact's engagement and CRM state (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/custom-buyer-journeys).
- **Key features:**
  - Define journey stages aligned to the sales funnel from cold prospecting to upsell
  - Stage-transition rules driven by CRM data (pipeline stage) and sales activity
  - Auto-rotate ad creative within a stage based on impression and click thresholds
  - Cross-channel triggers into Salesloft, Outreach, CRM, and MAP campaigns
  - Real-time sales notifications when a journey-tracked prospect engages
- **Source URL:** https://www.influ2.com/product/custom-buyer-journeys

### Cohort Designer (Cohort Builder)

> **Wiki:** [[influ2--cohort-designer]]

- **What it does:** Turns CRM data into target audiences for intent collection or ad targeting using sales activity, pipeline stages, and custom CRM fields (source: https://www.influ2.com/product).
- **Key features:**
  - Builds dynamic audiences from CRM fields, sales activity, and pipeline stage
  - Audiences feed both contact-level intent collection and contact-level ad targeting
- **Source URL:** https://www.influ2.com/product

### Signals Center

> **Wiki:** [[influ2--signals-center]]

- **What it does:** A sales-facing live feed that consolidates contact-level signals, CRM context, and recommended next steps so AEs and SDRs can craft outreach without tool-hopping (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/signals-center).
- **Key features:**
  - Live feed of recent buyer signals across the buying group
  - In-app context and "story" behind each signal to guide next action
  - Pulls company and account background from CRM into one view
  - Automatic notifications delivered where the seller already works
- **Source URL:** https://www.influ2.com/product/signals-center

### Revenue Reporting

- **What it does:** Quantifies pipeline created, progressed, and won attributable to ad engagement, even without form fills (source: https://www.influ2.com/product).
- **Key features:**
  - Pipeline created / progressed / won metrics by campaign and contact
  - Revenue attribution synced into CRM
  - Form-less attribution tied to per-contact ad engagement
- **Source URL:** https://www.influ2.com/product

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Self-styled "world's first Contact-Level Advertising platform" — pitches person-level ad targeting and reporting as the primary wedge against account-level ABM (source: https://www.influ2.com/contact-level-advertising).
- Ad engagement attributable to specific named individuals without requiring a form fill (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-ads).
- Cross-channel ad delivery automatically routed to where each named prospect is most active across LinkedIn, Facebook, Instagram, Google, Bing, and Amazon (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-intent).
- Self-reported metric: 2.18x pipeline conversion uplift for buying groups with at least one ad click vs. cold pipeline baseline (source: https://www.influ2.com/).
- Compliance posture: CCPA/GDPR-compliant, ISO 27001 certified, SOC 2 Type II attested (source: https://www.influ2.com/).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:** Influ2's Contact-Level Ads, Custom Buyer Journeys, and Cohort Designer compete head-to-head with **ZoomInfo Marketing** (ABM advertising orchestration) and the audience-building / advertising surfaces of **GTM Studio**. Influ2's Contact-Level Intent overlaps with the **Intent Data** layer that feeds ZoomInfo Marketing and the GTM Context Graph (source: https://www.influ2.com/product, https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-intent).
- **Adjacent overlap:** Signals Center is a seller-facing signal feed adjacent to **GTM Workspace**, but scoped only to Influ2-pipeline ad and intent signals rather than a unified seller surface across data, conversations, and CRM (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/signals-center). Contact-Level Intent's first-party social and search listening sits adjacent to **Chorus** in the broader signal-capture category (source: https://www.influ2.com/product/contact-level-intent).
- **No overlap:** Influ2 does NOT sell the underlying B2B contact/company database, technographics, phone/email coverage, CRM data quality (ZoomInfo Operations), or any developer-API / MCP / agent-builder play. Audiences come from "pre-built lists in your CRM" — Influ2 assumes the customer already has the data ZoomInfo otherwise sells (source: https://www.influ2.com/integrations).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ Both the G2 head-to-head page and any influ2.com /vs page returned blocked or unrelated content; SERP did surface a `vector.co` summary and a generic 6sense-vs-ZoomInfo article on influ2.com, but neither is a primary Influ2 vs. ZoomInfo comparison page authored by Influ2.

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "ABM that starts with people. Because people make decisions—not accounts." Influ2 helps marketers "connect, person to person" and gives sales "names, context, and timing — who to call and what to say" (source: https://www.influ2.com/).
- **Recurring proof points:** Customer quotes from Quantexa, Papaya Global, Amplitude, Optimove, Repsly, Paddle, EvaluAgent, Innova Solutions, Infinitus Systems; Quantexa case study cited on product page; Forrester report reference ("Forrester on Contact-Level ABM: Focus on Individual Buyers Within Accounts"); 2.18x pipeline conversion uplift metric; CCPA/GDPR/ISO 27001/SOC 2 Type II compliance.
- **Tone / category framing:** Frames itself as "Contact-Level ABM" and "Contact-Level Advertising," explicitly criticizing account-level ABM platforms as not "built for the messy reality of B2B sales."

## Summary

Bloomberg sells the Bloomberg Terminal — a financial-markets data, news, analytics and trade-execution workstation — into investment banks, asset managers, hedge funds, broker-dealers and corporate treasury / risk teams. It is tangential to ZoomInfo: there is no direct overlap with ZoomInfo's GTM platform, and Bloomberg's ICP (portfolio managers, traders, buy/sell-side analysts, investment bankers) does not intersect with ZoomInfo's sales / marketing / RevOps / GTM-engineer ICPs. The only shared adjacency is on the data + API layer — Bloomberg covers public-company financials and ships SDKs (BLPAPI, Excel Add-In, Server API), while ZoomInfo covers B2B contacts/companies/intent and ships its own enterprise APIs/MCP. Pricing is gated on Bloomberg's own site and is sourced here from Vendr and Wikipedia — typically $24K–$27K per terminal annually with two-year leases.

## Product Offerings

### Bloomberg Terminal

> **Wiki:** [[bloomberg--terminal]]

- **What it does:** Computer software system delivering real-time financial market data, news, analytics, messaging and trade execution to financial professionals across all asset classes.
- **Key features:**
  - Real-time market data and analytics across all asset classes (equities, fixed income, FX, commodities, derivatives, indices)
  - Bloomberg News and proprietary research
  - Instant Bloomberg messaging/chat across a network of 350,000+ decision makers
  - Bloomberg Launchpad customizable multi-window workspace
  - Multi-asset execution and order management integration
  - Bloomberg Professional App for mobile access (Android/iOS) and Bloomberg Anywhere for remote/web access via Citrix
  - Custom Bloomberg keyboard with biometric B-Unit authentication
  - Two-year lease cycles; ~325,000 subscribers worldwide as of 2022
- **Source URL:** https://professional.bloomberg.com/products/bloomberg-terminal/

### Bloomberg Intelligence (BI)

> **Wiki:** [[bloomberg--intelligence]]

- **What it does:** In-depth research and interactive data on industries, companies, government, credit, litigation and economics, delivered inside the Terminal by 500+ research professionals.
- **Key features:**
  - Coverage across 10 major sectors and 135+ industries
  - Over 2,000 company primers with financial fundamentals
  - Equity strategy, fixed income / currency / commodity (FICC) strategy, credit, ESG, government, and litigation research
  - Interactive charts blending Bloomberg proprietary data with 500+ third-party providers
  - Excel and API export of underlying data sets
  - Analyst-validated data sets
- **Source URL:** https://professional.bloomberg.com/products/bloomberg-terminal/research/bloomberg-intelligence/

### Data (Enterprise Data Catalog & Data Management)

> **Wiki:** [[bloomberg--data]]

- **What it does:** Enterprise data feeds and management products that deliver Bloomberg market, reference, pricing, regulatory, sustainable-finance and event-driven data into client systems.
- **Key features:**
  - Enterprise Data Catalog: Pricing Data, Reference Data, Regulatory Data, Real-Time Data Feed, Investment Research Data, Funds, Sustainable Finance, Event-driven feeds, Company Financials/Estimates Point-in-Time
  - Data License and Data License Plus for managed data delivery
  - Data Connectivity: Cloud Access, Server API, Swift Connectivity, Enterprise Console, Enterprise iPaaS
  - BLPAPI (Bloomberg Open API) SDKs for Python, Java, C/C++, .NET, Perl, Wolfram Language across Windows/Linux/macOS/Solaris
  - Excel Add-In ships with Terminal install for spreadsheet-based data retrieval
- **Source URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/

### Trading

> **Wiki:** [[bloomberg--trading]]

- **What it does:** Multi-asset electronic trading, order management and execution management products integrated with the Terminal.
- **Key features:**
  - Asset and Investment Manager (AIM) order management for buy-side
  - Trade Order Management Services (TOMS) for sell-side
  - Trade EMS execution management
  - Electronic Markets across Fixed Income, FX, Equity Listed, Emerging Markets
  - Trade Analytics including Transaction Cost Analysis (BTCA)
- **Source URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/

### Risk

> **Wiki:** [[bloomberg--risk]]

- **What it does:** Risk modeling, regulatory reporting and liquidity assessment products for financial institutions.
- **Key features:**
  - Multi-Asset Risk System (MARS)
  - MARS Hedge Accounting
  - Bloomberg Liquidity Assessment (LQA)
  - MAC3 Multi-Asset Class Factor Risk Modeling
  - Regulatory Reporting Services
- **Source URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/

### Compliance

- **What it does:** Compliance products for financial firms including communications archiving and trade surveillance.
- **Key features:**
  - Vault — communications archiving and surveillance
  - Transaction Cost Analysis (BTCA)
  - Order Management System integration
  - Regulatory Reporting Services
- **Source URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/

### Indices

- **What it does:** Bloomberg-branded benchmark indices spanning equity, fixed income, currency, commodities, multi-asset, ESG/climate, digital assets and thematic strategies.
- **Key features:**
  - Equity, Fixed Income, Commodities, Currency, Multi-Asset, ESG & Climate, Digital Assets, Thematic indices
  - Fixings & Reference Rates
  - Index Derivatives
  - Documentation and Benchmark Regulation resources
- **Source URL:** https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Closed proprietary network with ~325,000 Terminal subscribers and Instant Bloomberg messaging connecting 350,000+ decision makers — a switching-cost moat no GTM data vendor replicates (source: https://professional.bloomberg.com/products/bloomberg-terminal/)
- Coverage spans every asset class (equities, fixed income, FX, commodities, derivatives, indices) with real-time price feeds — depth that B2B sales-data vendors do not attempt (source: https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/)
- Bloomberg Intelligence: 500+ in-house research professionals covering 10 sectors, 135+ industries, 2,000+ company primers (source: https://professional.bloomberg.com/products/bloomberg-terminal/research/bloomberg-intelligence/)
- Bloomberg-branded indices and FIGI/LEI identifier issuance — Bloomberg owns the symbology layer of the markets it covers (source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomberg_Terminal)
- Premium price point ($24K–$27K/user/year) paired with two-year leases and 50–100% early-termination penalties — high commitment but stable pricing (2–3% annual increases) (source: https://www.vendr.com/marketplace/bloomberg)
- Custom Bloomberg keyboard with biometric B-Unit fingerprint authentication — physical hardware tied to user identity (source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomberg_Terminal)

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:** _No evidence in reviewed pages._ Bloomberg Terminal is a financial-markets workstation and does not compete head-to-head with any ZoomInfo product line (Data (Pillar 1) for B2B GTM, GTM Context Graph, GTM Workspace, GTM Studio, APIs & MCP for GTM agents, or any legacy module). The two products serve different ICPs with different data scopes.
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - _Data foundation (companies / financials):_ Bloomberg Intelligence and the Enterprise Data Catalog (Company Financials, Estimates and Pricing Point-in-Time) cover public-company financials, valuations and 2,000+ company primers — overlapping the company side of ZoomInfo's Data (Pillar 1), but Bloomberg's depth is investor-grade (fundamentals, debt, valuation) rather than GTM (contacts, intent, technographics) (source: https://professional.bloomberg.com/products/bloomberg-terminal/research/bloomberg-intelligence/).
  - _APIs & programmatic access:_ Bloomberg ships BLPAPI, Server API, Excel Add-In, Cloud Access and Enterprise iPaaS for programmatic data access — analogous in spirit to ZoomInfo's APIs & MCP, but the data being accessed is completely different (market prices vs B2B contact / company / intent) (source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomberg_Terminal).
- **No overlap:**
  - GTM Workspace and GTM Studio (seller / marketer / RevOps front-ends) — Bloomberg has no equivalent.
  - B2B contact data, direct-dial phones, verified business emails, technographics, intent data — Bloomberg sells none of these.
  - Conversation intelligence (Chorus equivalent) — Instant Bloomberg is counterparty messaging, not call recording / coaching.
  - ABM / marketing orchestration, sales engagement, CRM data quality / routing — none in Bloomberg's lineup.
  - Multi-asset trading and execution, MARS/MAC3 risk modeling, regulatory reporting, indices, communications surveillance (Vault) — these are core Bloomberg products with no ZoomInfo analog and sit entirely outside ZoomInfo's GTM platform scope (source: https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ Bloomberg-authored content names competitors as Refinitiv Eikon, FactSet, S&P Capital IQ, and Koyfin — never ZoomInfo or any B2B GTM vendor.

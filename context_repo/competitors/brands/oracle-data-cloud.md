# Oracle Data Cloud

## Summary

Oracle Data Cloud — the legacy ad-data marketplace built around BlueKai, Datalogix, and Crosswise — has been wound down; Oracle's active GTM-data product is now Oracle Fusion Unity Data Platform (Unity CDP), an enterprise customer data platform inside Oracle Fusion Cloud CX targeted at enterprise CMOs, RevOps, and IT teams in B2B and B2C industries. Unity unifies first-party customer data (orders, renewals, service history, ERP, behavioral signals) into a single governed profile and activates it through AI agents, journey orchestration, and 27+ ML models. Direct ZoomInfo overlap sits with **GTM Studio** (Oracle Fusion Marketing's agentic-orchestration pitch), legacy **ZoomInfo Marketing** (Unity's ABM segmentation), and the **GTM Context Graph** reasoning layer (Unity's Dynamic Profile Engine + Intelligence Workbench). Notably, Oracle's own Unity integrations page lists ZoomInfo as a B2B contact-data enrichment partner — meaning Oracle is a *consumer* of ZoomInfo data at the activation layer rather than a substitute for ZoomInfo's data foundation (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/unity-integrations/).

## Product Offerings

### Oracle Fusion Unity Data Platform (Unity CDP)

> **Wiki:** [[oracle-data-cloud--unity-cdp]]

- **What it does:** Enterprise customer data platform that unifies first-party customer data from sales, service, marketing, finance, ERP, and back-office systems into a single governed customer profile, then activates AI-driven audiences, journeys, and agentic workflows.
- **Key features:**
  - Unified Enterprise Data & Identity Resolution stitching front-office engagement with ERP, finance, data warehouse, and supply-chain records into real-time profiles
  - Dynamic Profile Engine modeling customers, accounts, buying groups, and products with relationship-aware profiles
  - Embedded Intelligence Workbench with 27+ ready-to-use AI/ML models including Next Best Action, Churn Propensity, Account Scoring, and Customer Lifetime Value; option to bring your own model
  - 100+ out-of-the-box behavioral attributes plus 80+ behavioral scores (engagement-by-channel, churn likelihood, purchase propensity)
  - Real-time activation and journey orchestration across email, SMS, push, paid media, and task creation
  - Out-of-the-box B2B, B2C, and B2B2C data models with industry-specific extensions
  - Native AI agents grounded in unified Fusion data (orders, renewals, financials, service history)
  - HIPAA, ISO 27001, and SOC 2 attestations; built natively on Oracle Cloud Infrastructure
- **Source URL:** https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/

### Oracle Fusion Marketing Orchestration

> **Wiki:** [[oracle-data-cloud--fusion-marketing]]

- **What it does:** AI-powered marketing suite that uses Unity data plus AI agents to detect buying/expansion signals (orders, renewals, usage, service history) and automatically launch the next-best play, including audience assembly, journey setup, handoffs to Fusion Sales, and reporting.
- **Key features:**
  - Agentic apps that turn enterprise signals into prebuilt audiences, messages, and revenue plays
  - Cross-team execution from sales, customer success, service, or partner workflows (not just marketing)
  - Eligibility, contract, region, and renewal-window guardrails on outreach to prevent out-of-policy targeting
  - Closed-loop measurement tying programs to Fusion Sales pipeline created, renewals protected, and expansion closed
  - Built-in approvals, brand rules, consent/privacy controls, and auditability
- **Source URL:** https://www.oracle.com/cx/marketing/

### Oracle Eloqua Marketing Automation

> **Wiki:** [[oracle-data-cloud--eloqua]]

- **What it does:** B2B marketing automation for campaign design, lead scoring, and integrated sales tools; runs on top of Unity-unified data.
- **Key features:**
  - B2B campaign design and execution
  - Lead scoring and nurture
  - Native integration with Oracle Sales/CRM
  - Eloqua-Unity integration for cross-channel orchestration
- **Source URL:** https://www.oracle.com/cx/marketing/

### Oracle Responsys Campaign Management

> **Wiki:** [[oracle-data-cloud--responsys]]

- **What it does:** B2C cross-channel campaign management platform integrated with Unity for activation across email, mobile, web, and ads.
- **Key features:**
  - Cross-channel B2C campaign orchestration
  - Email, SMS, push, and display activation
  - Integration with Unity-built audience segments
- **Source URL:** https://www.oracle.com/cx/marketing/

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- "Enterprise truth, not marketing truth" — Unity unifies orders, renewals, contracts, service, and finance with behavioral signals, claiming to anchor activation in real-time business reality rather than just clicks/web visits (source: https://www.oracle.com/a/ocom/docs/applications/cx/oracle-unit-cdp-datasheet.pdf)
- AI/ML runs natively in the Oracle database — no cloud-to-cloud data transfer required for predictive scoring, lookalikes, or recommendations (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/)
- Out-of-the-box B2B, B2C, and B2B2C data models with industry-specific extensions to accelerate deployment (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/)
- Forrester TEI study: 158% three-year ROI, $6.71M composite benefit, payback within seven months (Forrester-commissioned by Oracle, four-company composite) (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/forrester-tei/)
- Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader for Customer Data Platforms (cited 26 January 2026) (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/)
- Native agentic AI grounded in Fusion system-of-record data versus AI agents bolted on after the fact (source: https://www.oracle.com/a/ocom/docs/applications/cx/oracle-unit-cdp-datasheet.pdf)

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

This is the load-bearing section. Compare the competitor against ZoomInfo's positioning in `CONTEXT.md`.

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **GTM Studio (marketer/RevOps/GTM-engineer front-end of ZoomInfo)** vs. **Oracle Fusion Marketing Orchestration + Unity CDP** — Oracle Fusion Marketing uses "AI agents to watch for real buying and expansion signals... orders, renewals, product usage, service history" and "surfaces the next best plays," directly competing with GTM Studio's natural-language audience building and play orchestration (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/marketing/).
  - **ZoomInfo Marketing (legacy ABM module)** vs. **Unity CDP ABM segmentation** — Unity positions "granular segmentation for Account-Based Marketing (ABM) by firmographic data, past interactions, and behavioral insights" (source: https://blogs.oracle.com/cx/the-top-5-customer-data-use-cases-b2b-marketers-need-to-know).
  - **GTM Context Graph (reasoning across CRM + conversations + intent + behavior)** vs. **Unity Dynamic Profile Engine + Intelligence Workbench** — Unity claims relationship-aware profiles across customers, accounts, buying groups, and products with 27+ ML models (Next Best Action, Churn Propensity, Account Scoring); both platforms pitch a "why now" reasoning layer fused from operational + behavioral signals (source: https://www.oracle.com/a/ocom/docs/applications/cx/oracle-unit-cdp-datasheet.pdf).
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Operations (CRM data quality + routing)** vs. **Unity identity resolution + data quality** — Unity "cleanses and prepares" data with flexible ID resolution across devices, channels, and domains; adjacent to ZoomInfo Operations but Unity is broader (full enterprise consolidation) while ZoomInfo Operations focuses on CRM hygiene + B2B contact-data enrichment (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/).
  - **Data (Pillar 1)** vs. **Unity ingests ZoomInfo as a third-party enrichment partner** — Unity's integrations page lists ZoomInfo alongside Dun & Bradstreet and Experian as a B2B data enrichment provider, meaning Oracle is a *customer* of ZoomInfo data at the activation layer rather than a substitute for ZoomInfo's contact/company graph (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/unity-integrations/).
- **No overlap:**
  - B2C ecommerce, loyalty (CrowdTwist), service-agent enablement, field service, and ERP/HCM tie-ins — Oracle CX spans commerce, contact-center service, field-service routing, and ERP/HR, none of which ZoomInfo sells. Unity also serves heavy B2C industries (retail, hospitality, automotive consumer marketing) outside ZoomInfo's B2B GTM scope (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/platform/).
  - Legacy Oracle Data Cloud third-party audience marketplace (BlueKai/Datalogix DMP) was wound down and is no longer actively marketed; older PDFs (e.g., the 400M+ business profiles, 4K+ targeting options claim) remain indexed but reflect the discontinued business — Unity CDP is a first-party CDP, not a third-party DMP.
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ Oracle does not publish a head-to-head Oracle-vs-ZoomInfo page; the closest signal is Oracle listing ZoomInfo as an integration partner rather than a competitor.

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** Transform enterprise-wide customer data into actionable, trusted, AI-enabled customer profiles so enterprises can create and activate audiences, generate conversation-ready opportunities, personalize experiences, and optimize service operations (source: https://www.oracle.com/cx/customer-data-platform/).
- **Recurring proof points:**
  - Forrester TEI: 158% ROI over three years, payback within seven months
  - 20% boost in successful opportunities for a major telecom provider; 19% lead lift for a leading automotive brand; 230% revenue uplift for a major electronics retailer
  - Gartner Magic Quadrant Leader for Customer Data Platforms (Jan 2026)
  - Customer logos: Mazda Europe, Aegean Airlines, Aon, Frontier Airlines, Red Bull Racing, Visma, Cincinnati Bengals, Healthgrades
- **Tone / category framing:** "Enterprise customer data platform" as the data + AI backbone for all Oracle Fusion CX applications; positioned as "enterprise truth, not marketing truth" — the only CDP that unifies the system-of-record data the business runs on (orders, renewals, finance, service) with behavioral signals.

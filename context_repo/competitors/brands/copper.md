## Summary

Copper is a Google Workspace–native SMB CRM aimed at small service businesses (agencies, consulting, financial services) and sales leaders who already run on Gmail, Calendar, and Drive. Its product is the CRM of record itself — drag-and-drop pipelines, contact records, project pipelines, and lightweight email — sold transparently from $9 to $99 per seat per month. Versus ZoomInfo, the overlap is structurally adjacent rather than direct: Copper IS the kind of CRM that ZoomInfo Operations and the GTM Context Graph would enrich and route into, and Copper has no third-party B2B data, ABM, intent, conversation intelligence, or developer/agent ecosystem. The clearest crossover is at the seller-pipeline surface, where Copper's AI Pipeline Flags (first-party only) sit in the same UI slot as ZoomInfo's GTM Workspace prioritized accounts, but built on a fundamentally smaller and SMB-only data foundation.

## Product Offerings

### Copper CRM for Google Workspace

> **Wiki:** [[copper--google-workspace-crm]]

- **What it does:** Google-Workspace–native CRM that lives inside Gmail, Calendar, and Drive so users can add leads, track conversations, and manage tasks without leaving their inbox.
- **Key features:**
  - Native Gmail, Google Calendar, Google Contacts, and Google Drive sync — auto-captures contact info from email signatures and calendar invites
  - Google Chrome extension that brings Copper into Gmail and Google Calendar on desktop
  - Google Sheets add-on and Looker Studio integration for reporting (Professional tier and above)
  - LinkedIn 1-click contact add (all tiers)
  - AI email template generator and AI email re-writer (Professional tier and above)
  - "Recommended for Google Workspace" — the page describes Copper as the only CRM with this designation
- **Source URL:** https://www.copper.com/google-workspace-crm

### Pipeline Management

> **Wiki:** [[copper--pipeline-management]]

- **What it does:** Drag-and-drop visual sales pipelines with AI flags, automation, and forecasting designed for small sales teams.
- **Key features:**
  - Drag-and-drop sales pipelines with custom fields and stages
  - Pipeline automations that trigger emails or reminders when an opportunity changes stage
  - AI Pipeline Flags that "spot bottlenecks and surface high-value opportunities"
  - Workflow automations that auto-assign time-based tasks to teammates
  - Pipeline templates plus ability to create multiple pipelines (prospects, vendors, partners)
  - Real-time pipeline visibility, forecasting, and pipeline-health tracking
  - Activity feed for live view of contact and account activity
  - Custom fields on records and cards
- **Source URL:** https://www.copper.com/features/pipeline-management

### Projects & Service Delivery

> **Wiki:** [[copper--projects-service-delivery]]

- **What it does:** Lets teams duplicate closed deals into projects to handle service delivery, client onboarding, and ongoing client work — extending the CRM into post-sale workflow.
- **Key features:**
  - Customizable project pipelines and project pipeline templates
  - Project management views to track ongoing client work
  - Tasks and follow-ups assigned from a centralized list or within projects
  - Task and workflow automation to log calls, meetings, and activities
  - Cross-functional collaboration across sales, project managers, creatives, finance, and contractors
- **Source URL:** https://www.copper.com/

### Marketing & Outreach

> **Wiki:** [[copper--marketing-outreach]]

- **What it does:** Lightweight email outreach and re-engagement features layered onto the CRM record (deliberately not a full marketing automation platform).
- **Key features:**
  - Email templates and merge fields (all tiers)
  - Bulk email and email automation (Professional tier and above)
  - Email open and click tracking (click tracking on Business tier)
  - Email series / sequences (Business tier only)
  - "Rekindle" emails to automatically reconnect with dormant contacts
  - Contact forms for website lead capture
- **Source URL:** https://www.copper.com/pricing

## Target Audience & ICP

- **Industries called out:** Service businesses — agencies, consulting, financial services, real estate; "CRM for service businesses" is the homepage framing (source: https://www.copper.com/).
- **Company size called out:** SMB-first. The vs-HubSpot page explicitly contrasts Copper's small-business focus with HubSpot's "enterprise customers over small business needs" pivot (source: https://www.copper.com/resources/copper-crm-vs-hubspot).
- **Persona / role focus:** Small-business owners and founders, sales managers / sales leaders at SMBs, account managers, and project managers handling client delivery.
- **Use cases promoted:**
  - Sales pipeline management inside Gmail
  - Lead capture and contact enrichment from email signatures and calendar invites
  - Client onboarding and service-delivery project management
  - Customer relationship nurturing and re-engagement (Rekindle emails, follow-ups)

## Integrations & Ecosystem

- **CRMs:** Copper operates AS a CRM and does not integrate to other CRMs as a primary pattern. The HubSpot Marketing Hub integration is delivered via Zapier and treats HubSpot as the marketing automation, with Copper as the CRM of record (source: https://www.copper.com/integrations/hubspot-marketing-hub).
- **Sales engagement / outreach:** JustCall (cloud phone, dialer, AI call summaries), RingCentral, Slack, Mailchimp — all Professional tier and above (source: https://www.copper.com/integrations/justcall, https://www.copper.com/pricing).
- **Data / enrichment:** "Contact enrichment" is a Basic-tier feature limited to first-party signals — auto-capturing contact info from email signatures and calendar invites. No native enrichment from a third-party B2B contact database surfaced on reviewed pages.
- **Other notable integrations:** Google Workspace stack (Gmail, Calendar, Contacts, Drive, Sheets, Looker Studio, Gemini); DocuSign, PandaDoc, QuickBooks (contracts/proposals/billing, Professional+); Zendesk (Professional+); LinkedIn 1-click add (all tiers); Zapier with 5,000+ apps (all tiers); Dropbox (all tiers); Developer API and Embedded integration SDK (Professional+); iOS and Android mobile apps (all tiers) (sources: https://www.copper.com/pricing, https://support.copper.com/en/articles/9623758-copper-plans-feature-comparison).

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Only CRM with "Recommended for Google Workspace" status — natively lives inside Gmail and Google Calendar so users "never have to switch tabs" (source: https://www.copper.com/google-workspace-crm).
- Transparent, fully public pricing positioned as "no hidden costs," explicitly contrasted with HubSpot's free-then-upsell pattern (source: https://www.copper.com/resources/copper-crm-vs-hubspot).
- Single-product focus — "CRM is our one and only love" — vs. HubSpot's marketing-first sprawl now chasing enterprise (source: https://www.copper.com/resources/copper-crm-vs-hubspot).
- Complimentary onboarding and dedicated CSM access for qualified Business + Professional annual accounts with seat minimums (source: https://www.copper.com/pricing).
- 30,000+ customers and a portfolio of named SMB / service-business case studies (Tamlyn, OneSupport, Zivelo, Apptegy, Sajari, Jeni's Ice Cream, Creative Brands) (source: https://www.copper.com/).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:** _No evidence in reviewed pages._ Copper does not sell into any of ZoomInfo's primary product lines (Data foundation, GTM Context Graph, GTM Workspace, GTM Studio, APIs & MCP, Chorus, ZoomInfo Marketing). Copper IS a CRM of record, which is the destination for ZoomInfo data — not a substitute for it. _See flag._
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - Copper's pipeline-management surface (drag-and-drop pipelines + AI Pipeline Flags) sits in roughly the same seller UI slot as ZoomInfo Sales / GTM Workspace's prioritized accounts and AI-drafted outreach, but Copper drives prioritization only from first-party CRM signals — there is no third-party B2B data layer (source: https://www.copper.com/features/pipeline-management).
  - Copper's "Contact enrichment" Basic-tier feature is first-party only (email signatures, calendar invites), which sits adjacent to ZoomInfo Operations / the GTM Context Graph CRM-data fusion layer but is not a replacement for it (source: https://www.copper.com/pricing).
- **No overlap:**
  - **B2B data foundation** — Copper does not sell contact, company, phone, email, intent, or technographic data. The folk.app SERP entry summarizes the dynamic: Copper "fits ZoomInfo users when enrichment must translate into a CRM record" (source: https://www.copper.com/google-workspace-crm).
  - **Marketing / ABM / Studio** — Copper has bulk email and email series but no ABM, no intent data, no audience-building, and no marketing-orchestration platform. The vs-HubSpot page explicitly frames Copper as deliberately NOT a marketing automation platform (source: https://www.copper.com/resources/copper-crm-vs-hubspot).
  - **Conversation intelligence (Chorus)** — Copper itself does not record or analyze sales calls. JustCall integration adds AI call summaries and sentiment but is a partner integration, not a Copper product (source: https://www.copper.com/integrations/justcall).
  - **APIs & MCP / agent ecosystem** — Copper has a Developer API and Embedded integration SDK on Professional+ tiers, but no MCP server or AI-agent positioning surfaced on reviewed pages (source: https://www.copper.com/pricing).
  - **Negative-space — Copper covers ground ZoomInfo does not:** post-sale project / service-delivery pipelines for client onboarding and ongoing client work, aimed at agencies and service businesses (source: https://www.copper.com/).

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "Build better relationships and win more customers with Copper + Google" — a Google Workspace–native CRM that "lives in Gmail and Calendar" so SMB sales and service teams "never switch tabs."
- **Recurring proof points:** 30,000+ companies trust Copper; "Recommended for Google Workspace" (only CRM with this designation); Chrome Enterprise Recommended; customer logos for Campfire, MasterClass, Swell, Mailchimp, and SoftBank; named case studies including Tamlyn (a 50-year-old company's first CRM), OneSupport (173% growth), Zivelo, Apptegy, Sajari, Jeni's Ice Cream, and Creative Brands.
- **Tone / category framing:** "CRM software solutions company for service businesses" / "CRM for Google Workspace." Deliberately frames itself as a relationship CRM for SMBs, not a full GTM or marketing automation platform, and explicitly contrasts that focus against HubSpot's enterprise drift.

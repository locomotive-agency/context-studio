## Summary

Consensus is a Demo Automation / Product Experience Platform sold to presales (solutions consulting / sales engineering), sales, marketing, and customer success teams at B2B software companies. The product replaces repetitive intro/overview demos with on-demand interactive video demos, product tours, and full sandbox simulations, layering AI Agents and Demolytics-style stakeholder analytics on top. Crossover with ZoomInfo is **adjacent, not direct**: Consensus captures first-party *demo* engagement signals where ZoomInfo provides B2B data + GTM Context Graph + GTM Workspace/Studio surfaces — the two could be complementary in a GTM stack, but Consensus competes for the same presales/sales-enablement and pipeline-acceleration budgets that ZoomInfo's Workspace and GTM Studio target. There is no public Consensus-vs-ZoomInfo page; Consensus's own comparison content focuses on direct demo-automation rivals (Walnut, Reprise, Navattic, Demostack, Storylane).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:** _No evidence in reviewed pages._ Consensus does not sell B2B contact data, intent data, technographics, or any equivalent of the GTM Context Graph; it does not directly substitute for any ZoomInfo product line.
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **GTM Workspace + Chorus vs. Consensus Sales / Presales + Demolytics:** Consensus pitches itself as the buyer-engagement layer for sales reps and SCs (automated demos, stakeholder discovery, multi-threading, sales heatmaps). ZoomInfo's GTM Workspace prioritizes accounts and drafts outreach; Chorus captures call intelligence. Consensus captures *demo* intent rather than call/firmographic/intent signals — adjacent buyer-signal layer, not a substitute (source: https://goconsensus.com/solutions/presales-sales).
  - **GTM Studio vs. Consensus for Marketing:** Consensus for Marketing converts visitors into PQLs via embedded interactive tours and video demos and pushes that data into Marketo/HubSpot/Pardot. GTM Studio orchestrates audiences and plays from the GTM Context Graph; the two are arguably complementary (Studio targets the audience, Consensus runs the product experience) but compete for marketer pipeline-acceleration budget (source: https://goconsensus.com/solutions/marketing).
  - **APIs & MCP overlap:** Consensus exposes its own MCP endpoint on the Pro plan ("AI Agent Connect MCP Server" — "AI that talks to AI"). Both vendors are publishing into the agent-ecosystem MCP layer — Consensus publishes demo content / buyer signals; ZoomInfo publishes account, contact, and intent data. Adjacent in distribution layer, different in payload (source: https://goconsensus.com/pricing).
- **No overlap:**
  - ZoomInfo only: B2B contact data (500M contacts), company data, intent data, technographics, GTM Context Graph reasoning, SDR/outbound prospecting workflow, Account Management data layer, Developer / AI Agent Builder API distribution.
  - Consensus only: Interactive product tours, sandbox simulations, video demo automation, presales workflow tooling, Customer Success post-sale demo content, channel-partner enablement.
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ Consensus's footer "Comparison" menu links only to Walnut, Reprise, Navattic, Demostack, and Storylane — direct demo-automation rivals. See flag `vs_brand_missing`.

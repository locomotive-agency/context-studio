## Summary

Fathom is an AI meeting notetaker for SMB and mid-market customer-facing teams (1-200 employees per third-party comparison) that records, transcribes, and summarizes Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams calls — with sales-specific extensions for CRM sync and AI Scorecards. Direct overlap with ZoomInfo sits at Chorus: both produce AI summaries, action items, and CRM-synced call notes; Fathom adds sales coaching via methodology-graded scorecards. Fathom does not sell contact data, intent, or marketing orchestration, and explicitly positions itself as the lightweight, self-serve, free-tier alternative to enterprise conversation intelligence platforms. Buyer overlap is strongest at sales (AEs, managers, VPs) but Fathom skews toward individual reps and founder-led teams that ZoomInfo's enterprise GTM platform does not target.

## Product Offerings

### Fathom AI Notetaker (core platform)

> **Wiki:** [[fathom--ai-notetaker]]

- **What it does:** AI meeting recorder, transcriber, and summarizer for Zoom, Google Meet, and Microsoft Teams calls — available bot or bot-free.
- **Key features:**
  - Unlimited recordings and transcriptions
  - Choice of bot-free or bot capture (bot-free in beta on Mac)
  - Instant AI call summaries delivered to inbox
  - AI-generated action items and follow-up email drafts
  - Word-perfect transcripts handling multiple speakers and accents
  - Custom transcription dictionaries for company-specific vocabulary
  - Clips, playlists, and search across calls
  - Ask Fathom — natural-language Q&A across past calls
- **Source URL:** https://www.fathom.ai/overview

### Fathom for Sales (Sales & Success solution)

> **Wiki:** [[fathom--for-sales]]

- **What it does:** Sales-focused workflow that captures deal context, updates the CRM, and coaches reps with AI Scorecards.
- **Key features:**
  - Deal View summarizing insights across deal meetings
  - Customized call summaries by methodology
  - Automatic CRM field sync (pain points, timelines, decisionmakers)
  - AI Scorecards graded against chosen sales methodology
  - Coaching metrics for managers
  - AI search alerts (e.g. competitor mentions, pricing objections) via email or Slack
  - Ask Fathom across sales call history with cited answers
  - Native syncs to HubSpot, Salesforce, Slack, Asana, Close
- **Source URL:** https://www.fathom.ai/solutions/sales

### Fathom for Teams

> **Wiki:** [[fathom--for-teams]]

- **What it does:** Cross-functional workspace that creates a shared meeting source-of-truth across Sales, Marketing, Operations, HR, and Product teams.
- **Key features:**
  - Team recordings view, team folders, customer view, deal view
  - Account-wide Ask Fathom across all team meetings (beta)
  - Global keyword and attendee search across team meetings
  - Comments, mentions, keyword alerts, AI search alerts
  - Single sign-on (SSO)
  - Custom data retention policies (Business tier)
  - Launch Assist (expedited implementation, Business tier)
- **Source URL:** https://www.fathom.ai/solutions/teams

### Fathom for Marketing

> **Wiki:** [[fathom--for-marketing]]

- **What it does:** Marketing-team workflow for capturing campaign decisions, mining customer feedback, and turning calls into marketing assets.
- **Key features:**
  - Records, summarizes, organizes feedback and creative direction
  - Ask Fathom + AI Search across past calls for positioning and customer feedback
  - Sends decisions and tasks to project tools and CRM
  - Extracts quotes and story moments for content reuse
  - Shareable snippets for execs, agencies, cross-functional partners
- **Source URL:** https://www.fathom.ai/solutions/marketing

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

This is the load-bearing section.

- **Direct overlap:** Fathom's core notetaker plus AI Scorecards directly competes with **Chorus** — both record, transcribe, and analyze sales meetings; both produce AI summaries, action items, and CRM-synced call notes; both offer scorecard-based coaching. Third-party comparison frames them as the same category, with Chorus heavier on multi-call analytics and Fathom on speed/self-serve. (source: https://resources.rework.com/comparisons/ai-in-crm/gong-vs-chorus-vs-fathom)
- **Adjacent overlap:** Fathom Sales' Deal View + automatic CRM field sync + AI follow-up email drafts touch ZoomInfo's **GTM Workspace** seller front-end (deal context + AI-drafted outreach), but Fathom's surface is meeting-call-scoped only — no prioritized accounts, no first-party data foundation underneath. (source: https://www.fathom.ai/solutions/sales)
- **No overlap:** Fathom does NOT sell B2B contact data, company data, technographics, or intent — third-party review notes "Contact enrichment: No (not Fathom's lane)." Fathom for Marketing is meeting-decision capture and creative-quote mining; it does NOT compete with **GTM Studio** (audience building, ABM orchestration). No equivalent to ZoomInfo's Data (Pillar 1), ZoomInfo Operations (CRM data quality / routing), or developer-facing APIs & MCP at platform scale. (sources: https://resources.rework.com/comparisons/ai-in-crm/gong-vs-chorus-vs-fathom, https://www.fathom.ai/solutions/marketing)
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** No Fathom-authored vs-ZoomInfo page surfaced. Closest framing is third-party: "Chorus fits best when the organization is already deep in ZoomInfo and wants to avoid managing another vendor relationship. Fathom wins when the goal is simple: record meetings, get clean summaries, push action items to the CRM, and stop paying anything." (source: https://resources.rework.com/comparisons/ai-in-crm/gong-vs-chorus-vs-fathom)

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** AI meeting partner that captures every detail of your meetings so you can stay present, ask better questions, and never worry about missing something important — with automatic summaries, transcripts, and CRM sync.

- **Tone / category framing:** "AI meeting partner" / "AI notetaker" — explicitly the lightweight, self-serve, free-tier alternative to enterprise conversation intelligence (Gong, Chorus). Casual, productivity-tool tone vs. enterprise revenue-platform language.

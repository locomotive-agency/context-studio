# Artisan AI

## Sub-products

- [[artisan-ai--ava-bdr|Ava — the Autonomous AI BDR]] — competes with GTM Workspace
- [[artisan-ai--data|Artisan B2B Data + Watchtower]] — competes with Data (Pillar 1) / ZoomInfo Sales

## Summary

Artisan AI sells an autonomous AI sales agent ("Ava, the AI BDR") to founders, growth leads, and lean SDR/AE teams at startups, SMBs, and mid-market SaaS companies. The core overlap with ZoomInfo is end-to-end: Artisan ships a 250M–300M B2B contact database, intent signals, website-visitor de-anonymization, and multi-channel outreach inside a single autonomous agent — directly competing with ZoomInfo's data foundation plus the legacy ZoomInfo Sales/Engage cadencing surface (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-review). Artisan publishes a 4-tier price ladder ($0 / $250 / $600 / Custom) on a credit-based model and positions ZoomInfo as expensive, slow to onboard, and US-skewed. Where Artisan does NOT compete: conversation intelligence (no Chorus equivalent — Ava cannot legally make calls), GTM Context Graph–style cross-source reasoning, APIs & MCP, and ABM/marketing platform.

## Product Offerings

### Ava — the autonomous AI BDR

- **What it does:** Autonomous AI sales agent that sources leads, writes personalized emails, runs multi-channel sequences, handles replies, and books meetings on behalf of the sales team.
- **Key features:**
  - Searches 250M+ verified B2B contacts across 200+ countries
  - Enriches every prospect with intent signals like funding rounds and hiring activity
  - Ghostwrites hyper-personalized email sequences — "Every email is unique. No templates or variables"
  - Runs autonomous A/B (described as "A/Z") testing of subject lines, structure, CTAs, and tone
  - Handles replies, qualifies leads, handles objections, and books meetings directly on rep calendars
  - Multi-channel: email plus social outreach; queues phone-call steps in native dialer (Ava cannot legally make calls)
  - Adjustable autonomy: approval workflows, tone/CTA locks, budget caps, channel selection, lead-routing rules
  - Self-healing mailboxes and built-in deliverability suite
- **Source URL:** https://www.artisan.co/ai-sales-agent

### B2B leads + lead-finding (Find leads / B2B leads / Local data)

- **What it does:** Built-in B2B contact and company database with enrichment from 22+ data sources, including local-business and region-level data.
- **Key features:**
  - 250M+ verified B2B contacts in 200+ countries (source: https://www.artisan.co/ai-sales-agent)
  - 300M+ verified contacts including ecommerce and local businesses (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-alternatives)
  - Enrichment from 22+ data sources
  - Local-business and region-level data — "200M+ local businesses" (source: https://www.artisan.co/solutions/smbs)
  - Refreshes data with online activity signals like job changes and funding rounds (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-review)
  - CSV import/export and lists/enrichment workflow
- **Source URL:** https://www.artisan.co/

### Intent signals + Website visitor de-anonymization (Watchtower)

- **What it does:** Surfaces real-time buying signals (funding rounds, leadership hires) and identifies anonymous companies and people visiting the customer's website.
- **Key features:**
  - Real-time intent signals like funding rounds and leadership hires
  - Watchtower campaigns that monitor website visitors and spot intent (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-alternatives)
  - Website visitor de-anonymization including a person's email address — 5 credits per de-anonymization (source: https://www.artisan.co/pricing)
  - Signal-triggered campaigns on autopilot
- **Source URL:** https://www.artisan.co/

### Autonomous campaigns (cold outbound, CRM re-engagement, cross-sell/upsell)

- **What it does:** Three campaign motions Ava runs end-to-end — net-new cold outbound, MQL/closed-lost/churned re-engagement from CRM, and customer expansion (cross-sell / upsell).
- **Key features:**
  - Cold outbound: net-new prospects, signal-triggered, multi-channel (email/social, calls queued)
  - CRM motion: re-engages MQLs, conference leads, closed-lost deals, and churned accounts using CRM context
  - Cross-sell / upsell: account-aware personalization using CRM and product-usage data; sends on behalf of each account owner
  - Continuous A/B testing across copy variations
  - Sends from each rep's own email address so it looks like outreach from the company
- **Source URL:** https://www.artisan.co/

### Human power dialer + AI dialing copilot

- **What it does:** Native dialer add-on so human reps can call the leads Ava queues; AI copilot assists during calls.
- **Key features:**
  - Human power dialer at $67/seat/month (source: https://www.artisan.co/pricing)
  - AI dialing copilot included on higher plans
  - Ava builds the queue and surfaces talking points; reps make the actual calls
- **Source URL:** https://www.artisan.co/pricing

- **Company size called out:** Startups (dedicated /solutions/startups), SMBs (dedicated /solutions/smbs), Enterprise (dedicated /solutions/enterprise — present in nav, page not extracted, see flags)
- **Persona / role focus:** Founders without a full sales-ops function; Heads of Growth / Heads of B2B; BDRs / SDRs / AEs / AMs (homepage: "Trusted by 1,000s of BDRs, AEs, and AMs globally"); lean GTM teams scaling outbound without hiring more SDRs
- **Use cases promoted:**
  - Net-new cold outbound (signal-triggered, multi-channel)
  - Re-engaging closed-lost / churned / MQL CRM contacts
  - Cross-sell and upsell to existing customers
  - Event ticket sales, sponsorship outreach, and VIP reactivation (per SaaStr case study)
  - Replacing or augmenting a human BDR/SDR team at "1/5th the cost of a human BDR"

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Fully autonomous by default — Ava executes prospecting, outreach, replies, and meeting booking end-to-end without human prompting; positioned as "1/5th the cost of a human BDR" (source: https://www.artisan.co/ai-sales-agent)
- All-in-one outbound stack — B2B data, enrichment, signals, multi-channel sequencing, deliverability, and meeting booking inside a single agent — pitched as a replacement for stitching together ZoomInfo + Outreach + Salesloft (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-alternatives)
- "AI-employee" framing — Ava is marketed as a hire ("Hire Ava", "Skip the BDR team"), not a feature; customers can interview Ava live before signing (source: https://www.artisan.co/)
- Continuous autonomous A/B (A/Z) testing of subject lines, copy, CTAs, and tone — performance compounds without human optimization (source: https://www.artisan.co/)
- Transparent, public, low-end pricing ($0 / $250 / $600 / Custom) explicitly contrasted against ZoomInfo's quote-based enterprise model (source: https://www.artisan.co/pricing)
- Public proof: SaaStr case study reports 6,892 emails in 6 weeks, 3.55% positive reply rate, 49.41% social-media connection acceptance, and closed-won revenue from re-engagement (source: https://www.artisan.co/customers/saastr)

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Data foundation vs Artisan B2B leads / Find leads.** Artisan markets a self-built 250M-contact database with 22+-source enrichment and refresh via job-change / funding signals. Their /blog/zoominfo-review explicitly compares "ZoomInfo 260M+ (US-heavy) vs Artisan 300M+ (globally enriched, AI-refreshed)" (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-review).
  - **ZoomInfo Sales / Engage cadencing vs Artisan autonomous campaigns.** Artisan calls out ZoomInfo Engage by name as a "paid add-on" that "requires a good amount of setup" and "feel[s] a little outdated" next to AI-first sales engagement, then positions its autonomous-campaign engine as the replacement (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-review).
  - **ZoomInfo Buyer Intent + WebSights vs Artisan Watchtower / website visitor de-anonymization.** Artisan claims ZoomInfo's intent and visitor-ID products are "locked away as add-ons or inside ZoomInfo's more advanced … plans" while Artisan "includes real-time intent signal scraping and web visitor identification in all its plans" (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-review).
  - **GTM Workspace (seller front-end) vs Ava.** GTM Workspace surfaces prioritized accounts and AI-drafted outreach for sellers; Ava goes further by autonomously sending the outreach, handling replies, and booking meetings — competing for the same seller workflow but on the autonomous-execution side (source: https://www.artisan.co/ai-sales-agent).
- **Adjacent overlap:** ZoomInfo Operations (CRM data quality, routing) — Artisan syncs to Salesforce/HubSpot in real time and re-engages dormant CRM records but does not market routing or CRM data-quality as a standalone product (source: https://www.artisan.co/ai-sales-agent). GTM Studio (audience builder + play orchestration) — Artisan's three-motion campaign engine overlaps the orchestration surface for outbound use cases but does not target marketers/RevOps as primary buyers (source: https://www.artisan.co/).
- **No overlap:** Conversation intelligence (Chorus) — Ava explicitly cannot make calls (source: https://www.artisan.co/ai-sales-agent FAQ); GTM Context Graph — no equivalent cross-source reasoning fabric; APIs & MCP — no public API, MCP server, or developer surface in the reviewed pages; ZoomInfo Marketing / ABM platform — Artisan does not pitch advertising, ABM display, or demand-gen orchestration.
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** "ZoomInfo doesn't publish pricing. Most users report starting plans at $15K–$30K/year, making it one of the most expensive GTM platforms on the market … While ZoomInfo is a great sales prospecting and GTM app, it does have some major flaws … On the other hand, an AI sales tool like Artisan is more user-friendly. You can set it up in hours, not weeks. And once you've set it up, Artisan's AI SDR works on her own, without human intervention … something ZoomInfo is definitely not." (source: https://www.artisan.co/blog/zoominfo-review).

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** Hire Ava, the autonomous AI BDR — she finds leads, sends personalized outreach, handles objections, and books meetings at a fraction of the cost of a human BDR. "Outbound was hard to scale … with Ava, it's easy" (source: https://www.artisan.co/).
- **Recurring proof points:** SaaStr / Jason Lemkin ("we replaced our outbound sales team with Artisan; open rates doubled; closing $100K deals daily" — 6,892 emails in 6 weeks, 3.55% positive reply rate); SumUp / Karlo Biuk ("$52 cost per lead", "hundreds of thousands of emails sent"); CookUnity / Bruno Didier (scale a human-only team can't reach); Zirtual / Jenn Kussrow (3x higher reply rate, 81,785 emails in 3 months); press logos Forbes, NYT, TechCrunch, Inc., Business Insider, BBC; SOC2 / AICPA compliance.
- **Tone / category framing:** Artisan calls itself an "AI employees" / "AI BDR" / "AI sales agent" company — positioning to redefine outbound sales as a software workload rather than a labor workload. Tagline "Stop hiring humans" (referenced in their CEO's Reddit post surfaced in SERP). The category framing pivots the conversation from "sales intelligence platform" to "autonomous AI worker."

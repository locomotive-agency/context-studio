## Summary

Relevance AI is a no-code AI agent and multi-agent platform for GTM teams — pitched as "the home of the AI Workforce" — that ships both a horizontal builder (AI Agents, AI Workforce, Multi-Agent Systems) and a packaged AI BDR/SDR product called Bosh. Primary buyers are sales, RevOps, marketing, CS, and increasingly IT/AI Ops; named customers include Canva, Autodesk, KPMG, Databricks, Confluent, Lightspeed, Rakuten, and Freshworks. The most material overlap with ZoomInfo is on the universal-access pillar (APIs/MCP/agent-builder ecosystem) and on the AI SDR / GTM Workspace AI-agent motion (Bosh vs. ZoomInfo's AI-agent layer), with adjacent overlap into Operations (CRM enrichment, lead routing) and Studio (no-code play orchestration). Relevance AI does NOT sell a B2B data foundation or a GTM Context Graph and explicitly relies on third-party data sources (Apollo, LinkedIn, BuiltWith) — the area where ZoomInfo's three-pillar pitch is strongest.

## Product Offerings

### AI Agents (Agent Builder)

> **Wiki:** [[relevance-ai--ai-agents]]

- **What it does:** No-code platform for building autonomous AI agents that automate GTM tasks; "Build AI Agents to automate your GTM process. Use our no-code builders or our plugin with Claude Code & Codex." (source: https://relevanceai.com/agents)
- **Key features:**
  - No-code Agent Builder — connect apps, set triggers, define behavior
  - Use any LLM (Claude, OpenAI, Gemini) or bring-your-own LLM with credits
  - "Invent" — create custom AI agents by describing them in natural language
  - Knowledge / memory store and metadata capture per task
  - Scheduling, approvals, and escalation controls
  - Version control on every agent with rollback
  - Pre-built marketplace templates (Lead Research, Outbound BDR, Inbound Qualification, SEO, CRM Enrichment, Customer Support)
- **Source URL:** https://relevanceai.com/agents

### AI Workforce (Multi-Agent Builder)

> **Wiki:** [[relevance-ai--ai-workforce]]

- **What it does:** Visual builder that turns proven GTM playbooks into coordinated teams of AI agents (Workforces) that run end-to-end autonomously, triggered by events or signals (source: https://relevanceai.com/workforce).
- **Key features:**
  - Drag-and-drop visual canvas with triggers, handoffs, routing logic, escalation paths
  - Multi-agent systems where each agent owns a step and hands off context to the next
  - Encode playbook → agents run plays → continuous fine-tuning over time
  - Pre-built workforces: SDR, Inbound, Outbound, Onboarding, Expansion
  - Agent-to-agent feedback loops
  - Event/signal-triggered runs (e.g. "Daily at 9am", "Email reply received")
- **Source URL:** https://relevanceai.com/workforce

### Bosh — AI BDR / Sales Agent

> **Wiki:** [[relevance-ai--bosh]]

- **What it does:** A pre-built, customizable AI BDR/SDR agent that books meetings 24/7, runs inbound and outbound workflows, researches prospects, handles replies, and schedules meetings (source: https://relevanceai.com/bosh-sales-agent).
- **Key features:**
  - Always-on inbound and outbound SDR working around the clock
  - Researches prospects across LinkedIn, CRM, Yelp, Glassdoor, G2, Google, business registries, websites, enrichment services
  - Personalized outreach customized to your sales playbook
  - Handles replies, objections, and meeting scheduling on autopilot
  - Marketed as covering "Tier 4–5 accounts your team can't touch" and "increasing meetings per rep from 10 to 20"
  - Integrates with any tech stack
  - Trusted-by logos: Canva, Databricks, Confluent, Autodesk, Lightspeed, Rakuten, Freshworks, Aveva, Employment Hero, Qualified, ThoughtSpot
- **Source URL:** https://relevanceai.com/bosh-sales-agent

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **GTM Workspace (AI-agent layer + AI-drafted outreach + execution surface for sellers) vs. Bosh AI BDR/SDR + AI Workforce.** Bosh runs end-to-end inbound and outbound: research, personalised outreach, reply handling, meeting scheduling, CRM updates — directly competing with GTM Workspace's AI SDR motion and the prioritized-accounts + AI-drafted outreach value prop (source: https://relevanceai.com/bosh-sales-agent).
  - **APIs & MCP (universal access pillar) vs. Relevance AI Agents + Workforce + Programmatic GTM + public API.** Relevance AI sells the same "build agents that act on your stack" lane that ZoomInfo positions as Universal Access. Programmatic GTM is explicitly an MCP-based plugin for Claude Code and Codex, mirroring the agent-builder developer audience ZoomInfo's MCP server is positioned for (source: https://relevanceai.com/agents).
  - **ZoomInfo Operations (CRM data quality + enrichment + routing) vs. CRM Enrichment Agent + Inbound Qualification Agent + Lead Router agents.** Direct overlap with ZoomInfo Operations' data quality and routing motion (source: https://relevanceai.com/agents).
  - **GTM Studio (NL audience building + play orchestration for marketers/RevOps/GTM engineers) vs. AI Workforce + Multi-Agent Systems.** Workforce positions itself as "turn proven playbooks into autonomous AI workforces" with a visual builder for triggers and handoffs — same RevOps/GTM-engineer buyer GTM Studio targets (source: https://relevanceai.com/workforce).
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Marketing (ABM) vs. LIMA Lifecycle Marketing Agent + SEO Agent + Inbox Manager.** Touches marketing automation territory but is not a full ABM platform — no ad orchestration, no programmatic media, no audience-targeting infrastructure surfaced (source: https://relevanceai.com/agents).
  - **Chorus (conversation intelligence) vs. Phone Agent + meeting agent.** Relevance AI offers voice-calling and meeting agents but markets them as task-execution surfaces, not call-recording / coaching / intelligence (source: https://relevanceai.com/bosh-sales-agent).
- **No overlap:**
  - **B2B contact / company / intent / technographic data foundation.** Relevance AI is platform/agents only. Bosh explicitly relies on third-party data sources (Apollo, BuiltWith, LinkedIn, Glassdoor, G2, business registries) that the agent calls out, and Relevance AI does not sell its own first-party 500M-contact / 100M-company database (source: https://relevanceai.com/bosh-sales-agent).
  - **GTM Context Graph reasoning (proprietary intelligence layer fusing third-party data with first-party CRM + conversations + behavioral signals).** Relevance AI agents reason within their workflow but do not surface a unified "context graph" that explains why deals move; the platform treats CRM and conversations as integration endpoints, not a fused intelligence layer (source: https://relevanceai.com/).
  - **Conversation intelligence / deal intelligence / forecasting (Chorus-class).** No call recording, transcript analytics, or deal-risk forecasting product surfaced on extracted pages (source: https://relevanceai.com/).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public head-to-head Relevance AI vs ZoomInfo page surfaced from relevanceai.com._ The closest direct comparison content is a third-party page on `marketplace.relevanceai.com/compare/zoominfo-alternatives` ("Top 10 ZoomInfo Alternatives in 2026") that frames "Traditional databases like ZoomInfo maintain large, periodically updated contact directories" against "AI sales intelligence tools like the Relevance AI Sales Researcher" — i.e. positioning ZoomInfo as a static database and Relevance AI as a real-time AI agent layer (source: https://marketplace.relevanceai.com/compare/zoominfo-alternatives).

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "Scale GTM results, without scaling headcount." Build AI agents that run your GTM playbooks on autopilot — from BDR outreach to customer success.
- **Recurring proof points:** Customer logos Canva, Autodesk, KPMG, Databricks, Confluent, Lightspeed Commerce, Rakuten Advertising, Freshworks, Aveva, Employment Hero, Qualified, ThoughtSpot, Send Payments, SafetyCulture; outcomes named in testimonials ($7M pipeline at Qualified, 3x increase in meetings at SafetyCulture, 40 hours saved weekly at Send Payments); press in TechCrunch, Forbes, Fortune, The Information; analyst recognition from CB Insights, Everest Group, Capgemini, G2 (4.5 stars); SOC 2 Type II + GDPR compliance.

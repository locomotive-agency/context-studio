## Summary

Lead Forensics is a B2B website-visitor-identification vendor (UK + US presence) selling to B2B sales, sales ops, demand-gen, ABM, account-management, and agency teams who want to convert anonymous website traffic into named-company leads with appended decision-maker contacts. It overlaps directly with ZoomInfo's WebSights / signal-based prospecting capabilities and with the visitor-identification slice of ZoomInfo Marketing's intent stack — both reveal which companies are on a customer's website and route that to CRM/MA in real time. Where it does not compete: Lead Forensics has no general B2B contact universe (its decision-maker data is scoped to identified visitors only), no GTM Context Graph or conversation-intelligence reasoning layer, no GTM Workspace/Studio surfaces, and no public API or MCP. Pricing is fully gated behind "Book a demo" / free trial, and no public Lead Forensics-vs-ZoomInfo comparison page surfaced in primary-source SERP results.

## Product Offerings

### Lead Forensics Essential

> **Wiki:** [[lead-forensics--essential]]

- **What it does:** Entry-tier B2B website visitor identification for SMB — reveals the businesses visiting your website, surfaces business contact details for key prospects, and exposes the keywords driving traffic via the Lead Manager portal.
- **Key features:**
  - List of business visitors (company-level identification)
  - Business contact data for key prospects
  - Keyword / traffic source insight
  - Real-time notifications
  - Trigger reports and conversion tracking
  - Customizable dashboard via Lead Manager portal
  - Zapier-based integrations only (Mailchimp, Monday.com, Asana, etc.)
- **Source URL:** https://www.leadforensics.com/pricing/

### Lead Forensics Automate

> **Wiki:** [[lead-forensics--automate]]

- **What it does:** Enterprise tier that adds direct CRM integration, fuzzy-match deduplication, and orchestration logic to push identified visitors and contact data into existing sales/marketing workflows in real time.
- **Key features:**
  - Direct-API CRM integration with Salesforce, HubSpot, Microsoft Dynamics, Pipedrive, Zoho
  - Fully customizable workflows
  - "The Orchestrator" technology to sequence actions
  - "Fuzzy Matching" algorithm for CRM dedupe and clean ingest
  - Real-time alerts via email, Slack, or CRM
  - ICP matching and named-account watchlists for selective alerting
  - Decision-maker contact data appended to identified company records
  - Append-or-create logic against existing CRM/MA records
- **Source URL:** https://www.leadforensics.com/pricing/

### Website Visitor Identification engine (core technology)

> **Wiki:** [[lead-forensics--visitor-id-engine]]

- **What it does:** Core technology layer — a tracking snippet captures sessions and reverse-IP-matches them against Lead Forensics' proprietary database to return company-level records with firmographics, pages visited, session detail, and decision-maker contacts.
- **Key features:**
  - Reverse-IP tracking via lightweight on-site snippet
  - Proprietary IP-to-company database, "built and maintained solely for their solution" (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/)
  - Real-time alerts when a company visits — including pipeline accounts re-engaging
  - ICP and named-account watchlists
  - Decision-maker contact data supplementing each identified company
  - CRM/MA integration for routing identified visitors to existing accounts or creating new records
  - Match rate explicitly tied to traffic mix (stronger on enterprise/mid-market corporate-network traffic; weaker on residential/SMB/remote-worker traffic)
- **Source URL:** https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/

### B2B Sales solution (use-case packaging)

> **Wiki:** [[lead-forensics--b2b-sales-solution]]

- **What it does:** Sales-team-oriented packaging — real-time alerts when pipeline accounts revisit, ICP segmentation of website traffic, win-back identification of lapsed customers, and decision-maker contact data pushed to reps' existing channels (email, Slack, CRM).
- **Key features:**
  - Real-time alerts when pipeline opportunities revisit the website
  - Lapsed-customer win-back identification (claim: "80% chance" of win-back when a lapsed customer revisits)
  - ICP/firmographic segmentation of website visitors (geo, employee count, industry, turnover)
  - Decision-maker contact data pushed to reps
  - Engagement-criteria-based alert prioritization via CRM integrations
- **Source URL:** https://www.leadforensics.com/services/b2b-sales-and-sales-operations/

### B2B Marketing solution (use-case packaging)

> **Wiki:** [[lead-forensics--b2b-marketing-solution]]

- **What it does:** Marketing-team packaging — convert the 98% of unconverted website traffic into named-company first-party intent data, fuel ABM, attribute PPC spend at company level, and route identified visitors into marketing automation for nurture.
- **Key features:**
  - First-party intent data on the 98% of visitors who don't fill a form
  - ABM target-account real-time alerting and engagement tracking
  - PPC ROI attribution at company level (not just landing-page form conversions)
  - Marketing-automation integration for nurture workflows
  - Append-or-create logic into CRM/MA via the Automate plan
  - RFP/tender visit detection on the customer's site
- **Source URL:** https://www.leadforensics.com/services/b2b-marketing-website-insight-lead-forensics/

## Differentiators (vs the broader category)

- Proprietary IP-to-company database "built and maintained solely for their solution," explicitly contrasted with "most tools" that pull from third-party sources and "aren't in control of that data quality" (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/).
- Real-time identification "at the moment a company visits, not at the end of the day or the end of the week" — framed as material for response-time-sensitive outbound (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/).
- Decision-maker contact data appended natively to each identified company so SDRs go straight from identification to outreach without a separate prospecting step (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/).
- Automate tier adds "The Orchestrator" action sequencing and a "Fuzzy Matching" dedupe algorithm specifically for keeping CRM data clean during high-traffic enterprise ingest (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/integrations/).
- Free 14-day trial used as the primary sales motion — pricing page contains zero dollar amounts; every CTA is "Book a demo" or free trial (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/pricing/).
- Self-positioning as "#1 in B2B Website Visitor Identification" with named ABM outcomes — Hotwire $100M pipeline, Travelers +126% opportunities / +£136K, Bay MarketForce 5x appointment rate, SugarCRM 20x ROI, RH Commercial Vehicles 1,500% ROI / +£50K (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/, https://www.leadforensics.com/case-studies/hotwire).

## Crossover With ZoomInfo

- **Direct overlap:**
  - **WebSights / signal-based prospecting (legacy ZoomInfo Marketing + GTM Workspace surfaces) ↔ Lead Forensics Essential + Automate.** Both add company-level identity to anonymous website traffic and route real-time alerts to CRM. Lead Forensics' core pitch — "reveal which businesses are clicking around a website," real-time alerts, ICP watchlists — is the same job-to-be-done as WebSights (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/).
  - **Data (Pillar 1) (decision-maker contacts) ↔ Lead Forensics' appended decision-maker data.** Lead Forensics "supplements identified company records with the names and contact details of key decision-makers within the visiting organization" — same data plane as ZoomInfo's contact data, but scoped only to companies that visited the customer's site, not the broader 500M-contact universe (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/).
  - **ZoomInfo Marketing — ABM and intent surfacing ↔ Lead Forensics ABM use case.** Lead Forensics explicitly markets itself as fueling ABM with "real-time intent data" and identifying when named target accounts visit; Hotwire case study claims $100M in ABM pipeline revenue (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/services/b2b-marketing-website-insight-lead-forensics/, https://www.leadforensics.com/case-studies/hotwire).
- **Adjacent overlap:**
  - **ZoomInfo Operations (CRM data quality + routing) ↔ Lead Forensics' "Fuzzy Matching" + "Orchestrator" on the Automate plan.** Lead Forensics performs append-or-create logic and fuzzy-match dedupe across Salesforce / HubSpot / MS Dynamics — but only on the slice of records sourced from website visitors, not the full CRM-hygiene scope of ZoomInfo Operations (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/pricing/).
  - **ZoomInfo Marketing — third-party intent data (Forrester Wave Leader Q1 2025) ↔ Lead Forensics first-party intent.** Lead Forensics positions itself as a complement, not a substitute: "visitor identification operates at a different point in the journey: it confirms when a company has reached your website specifically, making the signal higher-confidence because it reflects direct engagement with your brand rather than general category interest" (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/website-visitor-identification/).
- **No overlap:**
  - **GTM Context Graph and Chorus conversation reasoning** — Lead Forensics has no conversation-intelligence layer, no first-party CRM/conversation reasoning, and no "why deals move" narrative. Reverse-IP only.
  - **GTM Workspace / GTM Studio surfaces** — Lead Forensics ships an alerting-and-routing surface ("Lead Manager portal") and CRM push, not a generative seller workspace or marketer/RevOps audience-building studio with AI-drafted outreach or natural-language audience builders.
  - **Broad B2B contact universe** — no general-prospecting database; Lead Forensics' contact data is only for decision-makers at companies that have already visited the customer's site.
  - **APIs & MCP / agent-builder ecosystem** — no public API or MCP surface mentioned on reviewed pages; integrations are CRM and Zapier only (source: https://www.leadforensics.com/integrations/).
- **Their pitch against ZoomInfo (if found):** _No public comparison page surfaced in SERP results._ G2, TrustRadius, SoftwareAdvice, Capterra and others publish third-party Lead-Forensics-vs-ZoomInfo comparisons, but no Lead-Forensics-authored vs/comparison page was returned by the SERP queries.

## Messaging & Positioning Notes

- **Top-line value prop (their words):** "World's #1 B2B website visitor identification" — uncover the 98% of B2B website visitors who don't fill in a form, get their decision-maker contact data, and feed real-time alerts into the channels and CRMs your team already uses.
- **Recurring proof points:** Hotwire ($100M ABM pipeline, 100% target-market engagement, 75 C-level contacts); Travelers (33,000 visitors matched in a year, +126% opportunities, +£136,000 revenue); Bay MarketForce (5x appointment rate, 39% vs 7% baseline); SugarCRM (sub-5-minute response, 20x ROI); RH Commercial Vehicles (1,500% ROI, +£50,000 revenue); self-billed "#1 in B2B Website Visitor Identification."
- **Tone / category framing:** Frames itself as "B2B website visitor identification" — a distinct category from contact databases (ZoomInfo, Apollo) and from third-party intent (Bombora, 6sense). Explicitly contrasts itself with Google Analytics ("GA4 anonymizes by design"), cookie-based marketing-automation tracking (only works after a form fill), and third-party intent data ("higher-confidence because direct engagement, not general category interest").

### Voice Calibration

**Emotional state:** Skeptical and evaluation-mode by default — they have been burned by vendors who oversold data quality or integration simplicity, so they are actively looking for technical specificity, architecture diagrams, and proof that the platform will not create new maintenance debt while solving old problems.

**Messaging angle:** Write for someone who has already been burned by a vendor that promised 'clean data' and delivered a CSV import — lead with architecture and specificity, not outcomes, and let the technical proof points earn the business case.

**Tone modifiers:**

- Technical and specific — name the architecture, not just the outcome
- Respect the expertise — do not over-explain concepts this persona already owns
- Acknowledge the maintenance debt problem before pitching the solution
- Use systems-thinking language: pipelines, layers, dependencies, failure modes
- Cite sources inline — this persona will verify claims independently
- Avoid marketing superlatives; let proof points carry the weight

### Pain Points

**Primary pain:** CRM data is perpetually incomplete, stale, or inconsistent — and every enrichment, routing, or automation workflow built on top of that foundation inherits the same gaps, forcing RevOps to spend engineering cycles on data hygiene instead of building GTM leverage.

**CRITICAL** | Incomplete and decaying CRM data undermines every downstream workflow *(also affects: sales_ae_sdr, marketing_demandgen)*
- *"We have 40,000 accounts in Salesforce and I'd estimate maybe a third of them have accurate firmographics. The rest are missing industry, wrong employee count, or the contact left two years ago. **CRITICAL** | Multi-vendor enrichment stitching creates brittle, expensive infrastructure *(also affects: sales_ae_sdr, marketing_demandgen)*
- *"Right now I'm managing three separate enrichment vendors — one for contact data, one for firmographics, one for intent. Each has its own API contract, its own data format, its own failure mode. When one breaks, the whole pipeline breaks and I'm the one debugging it at 9pm."*

**MAJOR** | Engineering bottlenecks block GTM teams from launching plays independently *(also affects: marketing_demandgen)*
- *"Marketing wants to launch a new ABM segment and I have to write a SOQL query, build a flow, test it in sandbox, and get it through change management. That's a two-week cycle for something that should take an afternoon. They end up just emailing me a spreadsheet and asking me to upload it manually."*

**MAJOR** | Slow lead routing and speed-to-lead degradation costs pipeline *(also affects: sales_ae_sdr, marketing_demandgen)*
- *"Our inbound leads sit in a queue for 15-20 minutes before they're enriched, scored, and routed to the right rep. By the time the rep gets the notification, the prospect has already moved on. I know this is a problem but fixing it means rebuilding the entire enrichment step in the routing flow."*

**MAJOR** | No unified view of account health signals across first-party and third-party data *(also affects: account_mgmt_cs, marketing_demandgen)*
- *"We have intent data in one tool, product usage in another, CRM activity in Salesforce, and conversation data in Chorus. Nobody has connected these. Leadership asks me for a churn risk model and I'm manually pulling CSVs from four systems and trying to join them in Python. That's not scalable."*

**MODERATE** | Territory and TAM models built on stale snapshots go stale immediately *(also affects: sales_ae_sdr, marketing_demandgen)*
- *"We do territory planning once a year and by Q2 the underlying data is already wrong — companies have grown, contacts have churned, new accounts have entered our ICP. - Build a unified account intelligence layer that connects first-party CRM and conversation data with third-party signals for scoring, routing, and forecasting

### Decision Drivers

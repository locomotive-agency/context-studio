# GTM Context Graph - The Context Layer for GTM

**Source:** [https://gtm.ai/](https://gtm.ai/)

**Canonical reference:** This is the authoritative wiki node for the GTM Context Graph (also referred to as the Context Layer for GTM, the Context Graph, or GTM AI). All content optimizations referencing the intelligence/reasoning layer should link here and to the gtm.ai product landing page.

## Summary

The GTM Context Graph is the intelligence layer that captures not just what happened in your GTM motion, but why. A semantic graph that fuses ZoomInfo's verified B2B data with customer CRM data, conversation intelligence, and behavioral signals into a unified, queryable structure that AI can reason over. Processes 1.5B+ data points daily to make every account, contact, and signal AI-readable across Claude, ChatGPT, and custom agent platforms.

Where CRMs record state changes, the GTM Context Graph captures the causal chain: entities, engagements, signals, and relationships connected in a machine-readable structure so AI tools understand why deals progress, not just that they did.

## Key Capabilities

- Semantic graph connecting accounts, contacts, engagements, signals, and AI artifacts into a unified, queryable structure
- Captures temporal patterns, engagement velocity, and semantic meaning beyond traditional entity-relationship models
- Four integrated platform layers:
  - **Data Foundations** — 100M+ companies with attribute-level confidence scoring
  - **Matching Engine** — entity resolution across first-party and third-party sources
  - **Context Graph** — connects entities, relationships, and patterns across systems
  - **AI Models and Agents** — federated models for account planning, deal scoring, and pipeline forecasting
- Universal access through MCP (Model Context Protocol), REST APIs, and native integrations with Claude, ChatGPT, Salesforce, and more
- Processes 1.5B+ data points daily across 28M site domains scanned, 210M IP-to-organization pairings, and 30,000+ tracked technologies
- Marketplace of pre-built data sets, audiences, and AI agents (franchise owners, fleet data, UCC filings, ready-to-use targeting segments)

## The GTM Hierarchy

> **Context > Timing > Targeting > Content**

Four forces govern every GTM motion. They are sequential and hierarchical: each one depends on the one above it, and the returns compound in order. The Context Graph exists because **Context is the foundation everything else builds on**. Use this hierarchy to frame why a strong context layer is the prerequisite for AI-driven GTM, not an optional upgrade.

| Rank | Force         | What it is                                                                                                                                                                                          | Hard truth                                       |
| ---- | ------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------ |
| 1st  | **Context**   | The data foundation: grounding data, unified identity, a connected context graph. An AI model is only as intelligent as the context feeding it. Without structured context, every downstream GTM motion flies blind. | Context is the foundation everything else builds on. |
| 2nd  | **Timing**    | With the right context, you reach accounts at the moment they're ready to engage. Triggers: intent signals, funding events, leadership changes, technology evaluations, contract renewals.          | You can't time your way out of bad context.     |
| 3rd  | **Targeting** | Precise targeting depends on context (who to reach) and timing (when to reach them). The best ICP models combine firmographic fit, technographic alignment, intent signals, and engagement history. | You can't target your way out of missed timing. |
| 4th  | **Content**   | Personalized content is the final mile. A message powered by deep context, good timing, and precise targeting feels like someone did their homework. Same template on a bad list? Spam.             | Great content can't fix sloppy targeting.       |

**Source:** [The GTM Laws of Physics](https://gtm.ai/guides/the-gtm-laws-of-physics) (gtm.ai guide, ~19 min read).

GTM AI is the context foundation for AI-powered go-to-market. It combines proprietary B2B data, entity resolution, a semantic context graph, and federated AI models into a single platform that gives your AI tools the context they need to drive revenue outcomes.

CRMs record state changes. GTM AI captures the causal chain. Instead of flat records, the Context Graph connects entities, engagements, signals, and relationships in a machine-readable structure that AI can reason over. This means your AI tools understand why deals progress, not just that they did.

The Context Graph is a semantic layer that connects accounts, contacts, engagements, signals, and AI artifacts into a unified, queryable structure. ### Q: How do I integrate the Context Graph with my existing tools?

The MCP server lets any AI agent query the Context Graph directly. See the [[zoominfo-mcp|ZoomInfo MCP]] product node for setup details, or the [MCP docs](https://gtm.ai/docs/mcp/getting-started).

### Q: What is the GTM AI Marketplace?

The Marketplace offers pre-built data sets, audiences, and AI agents that you can use out of the box. Data sets give you access to specialized data (franchise owners, fleet data, UCC filings, and more). Audiences are pre-defined targeting segments ready for outreach.

# Synthetic Sales Conversation: Summit Retail Competitive Switch

Date: 2026-06-11
Account: Summit Retail Group
Segment: Omnichannel retail
Participants: Grace Allen, CMO; Victor Chen, Marketing Technology; Heather Young, Legal Operations; Morgan Blake, Seller

## Summary

Summit is considering replacing a homegrown prompt library and shared-drive search process. The buying team wants a simpler way to make AI-generated campaign and sales content consistent.

## Transcript Excerpt

Morgan: What is not working with the prompt library?

Grace: The prompts are fine. The problem is context. People attach different source files, and the same campaign request produces different claims depending on who ran it.

Victor: We have a shared-drive search tool, but it returns everything. Draft positioning, customer interviews, old campaign briefs, legal notes. It is useful research, but it is not governed context.

Heather: My team does not want to approve hundreds of things one by one. We need a small set of controlled legal records and a way to keep raw sources visibly raw.

Morgan: What would you test first?

Grace: A campaign brief for enterprise operations leaders. I would want approved messaging, value proposition, proof points, and supporting buyer language from transcripts.

Victor: I would test permissions too. Admins manage users, editors manage the knowledgebase, viewers can request context. That is enough.

Heather: The phrase I care about is "supporting source." If the UI says supporting source and gives a citation, people understand it is not the official claim.
